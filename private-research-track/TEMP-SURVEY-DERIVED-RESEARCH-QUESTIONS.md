### Status and evidential limits

This section was mined after collection from the blinded `YUE-JUDGMENT-PILOT-01` survey export dated 2026-07-24 07:22.

It is a **private research-question queue**. The response patterns motivate questions and follow-up contrasts; they do not establish unrestricted productivity, authorize a parser label, change a construction status, or replace independent linguistic research.

Aggregate basis:

- 65 exported records;
- 49 eligibility-qualified respondents;
- 44 qualified respondents with at least one rating;
- 24 qualified respondents who completed all assigned items;
- complete-sample condition totals: `n = 24` per condition;
- regional weighting among qualified respondents: 42 Hong Kong, 4 Guangzhou, 2 Macau, 1 other/uncertain.

The current panel is therefore strong enough to identify contrasts worth researching, but not to settle regional scope. Free-text repairs are treated as hypothesis-generating evidence rather than authoritative analyses.

### Survey-derived state

- **survey-derived boundary question** — the survey reveals a potentially meaningful boundary inside or adjacent to an existing construction family.

- **survey-derived compositional audit** — the result may be ordinary composition rather than a new construction; test this before proposing a dedicated label.

### Methodological questions generated by the survey

1. **Regional replication:** How much of the variation, especially for `V完咗O`, reflects Hong Kong versus Guangzhou or Macau Cantonese? The present qualified sample is too Hong Kong-heavy to answer this.
2. **Lexical-versus-structural rejection:** Do choices such as `程式/app/軟件`, `車飛/車票/飛`, `溫書/溫習`, `生果/水果`, `擺/放`, and `空時間/預留時間` change ratings independently of the tested construction?
3. **Interpretation before correction:** For potentially ambiguous strings, does a forced interpretation question explain ratings better than free-text repairs alone?
4. **Matched lexical frames:** Can each follow-up keep the verb, object, discourse context, and orthography constant while manipulating only the target form?
5. **Partial-response handling:** Do complete and partial qualified respondents show the same condition ordering after item-level and respondent-level modelling?
6. **Variety-sensitive orthography:** Which written forms are broadly acceptable across varieties, and which should be presented as counterbalanced lexical variants rather than fixed wording?
7. **No direct implementation inference:** Even a near-ceiling condition must still pass independent-source, collision, corpus, boundary, and held-out review before any parser or status decision.
