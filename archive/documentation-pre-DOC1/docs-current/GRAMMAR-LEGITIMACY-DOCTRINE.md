# Grammar-Legitimacy Doctrine

> **Current authority — 2026-07-17 / CP020R2-RA1:** Accepted parser behavior is `v0.5.174-lane01-rendered-spatial-kinship-role-correction-r2`; there is no active candidate. `AUTHORITY-ORIGIN-AND-CLAIM-PROVENANCE.md` is binding. Legacy claims remain quarantined unless source-linked; future language claims must emerge from independent external evidence before parser design. Rendered acceptance does not establish productivity.

## Doctrine 1 — Parser success is not grammatical truth

A parser can produce stable, full-root, regression-preserving output and still implement the wrong analysis. Implementation evidence and language evidence are separate.

## Doctrine 2 — Historical acceptance preserves behavior, not legitimacy

Accepted fixtures remain regression obligations unless deliberately changed later. They do not automatically validate the construction label, hierarchy, productivity, or learner explanation.

## Doctrine 3 — Labels are not automatically linguistic constructions

A runtime label may denote:

- a supported Cantonese construction;
- a provisional analysis;
- a parser wrapper;
- an ambiguity container;
- a heuristic selection result;
- a lexicalized phrase;
- a dormant registry entry.

Each must be identified honestly.

## Doctrine 4 — Evidence must be pattern-specific

Support for a broad family does not transfer automatically to every subtype or wrapper. Sources must support the actual form, meaning, scope, and distribution being claimed.

Source counts, bibliographies, and lane-level citations do not satisfy this doctrine. Each material claim must have explicit claim-source edges with canonical locators, support direction, exact scope, and limitations.

## Doctrine 4A — Authority origin precedes the claim

Future Cantonese language claims must be written after independent source propositions have been extracted. Internal parser behavior may open a neutrally worded research question but may not provide a hypothesis, category, boundary, or preferred answer.

Compatible evidence found after a legacy internal claim is `legacy_source_linked_posthoc`, not proof that the claim was externally originated.

## Doctrine 5 — Productivity requires boundaries

A productive claim requires cross-lexeme evidence, negative controls, ambiguity cases, held-out evaluation, and restriction tracking. Positive examples alone are insufficient.

## Doctrine 6 — Naturalness and analysis are distinct

A speaker may accept a sentence without endorsing the parser analysis. Naturalness evidence cannot validate hierarchy, roles, omitted arguments, or productivity.

## Doctrine 7 — Competing analyses must remain visible

When evidence cannot choose reliably, keep the result provisional, review-bearing, or deferred. Do not hide theoretical uncertainty behind a clean label.

## Doctrine 8 — Corpus evidence is contextual

Corpus hits establish attestation only after manual context review. Search matches and automated dependencies are candidate-generation tools, not conclusions.

## Doctrine 9 — Legacy quarantine and authority-origin expansion are separate

Legacy claims without pattern-specific external edges remain downgraded, quarantined, lexicalized, representation-only, or retirement candidates. They cannot use parser behavior, fixtures, or historical acceptance as positive grammar evidence.

The former indefinite freeze and one-for-one replacement requirement are retired because they can block genuine source-emergent coverage and encourage artificial replacement mappings. A new construction need not replace an unrelated rejected label. It is eligible for a separate implementation batch only after a neutral question, recoverable source-screening log, proposition extraction, contradictory/boundary evidence, source-derived synthesis, explicit claim-source edges, immutable evaluation, competing-analysis review, and recovery checkpoint exist.

Research never changes runtime status implicitly. Replacement remains appropriate when the evidence directly supersedes an invalid claim, but it is no longer the only admissible path.

## Doctrine 10 — Recovery is part of scientific validity

An undocumented or undownloadable finding is not durable project knowledge. Every substantive batch must begin with an in-progress checkpoint and export at question freeze, source-screen freeze, proposition-extraction completion, synthesis, and pre-implementation boundaries. A large coherent batch may not remain unexported until its end.

## Doctrine 11 — Regression is a detector, not a truth constraint

Accepted fixtures preserve observable behavior for comparison. When independent evidence contradicts them, the project may deliberately retire or rebaseline the expectation with a recorded adjudication. A green regression count cannot justify false grammar.
