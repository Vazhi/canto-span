## Exact research question

Does contemporary Cantonese support a productive overt-head nominal schema consisting of an overt proximal or distal demonstrative, an overt classifier, and an overt nominal head, with no overt numeral and no inserted hidden material?

## Candidate construction

**Internal candidate name:** `OvertHeadDemonstrativeClassifierNP`  
**Compatibility name:** `DemonstrativeClassifierNP` only after the broad existing label is prevented from covering headless forms.

### Required overt structure

`demonstrative + classifier + nominal_head`

### Included

- proximal and distal demonstratives;
- multiple independently licensed classifiers;
- human, artifact, organization, animal, vehicle, food, and substance heads where lexically supported;
- role-neutral NP use. Subject, object, topic, predicate nominal, or fragment status belongs to the containing construction.

### Excluded

- headless `D + CL`;
- overt `D + numeral + CL + N`;
- `D + 啲 + N`;
- numeral-classifier NPs without a demonstrative;
- classifier-noun NPs without a demonstrative;
- wh-classifier phrases;
- demonstrative plus noun without an overt classifier;
- hidden `一` or hidden noun;
- a universal definiteness, referentiality, dialect, or discourse-function claim;
- non-deictic/filler uses whose constituency is unresolved.

## Source-originated propositions

1. Cantonese grammars and formal analyses distinguish an overt D-(X)-C-N schema from X-C-N, C-N, and N. This supports an overt-token construction family but not one universal syntactic theory.
2. D-C-N and D-Num-C-N are distinct overt profiles. The latter must not be generated by inserting a silent numeral into the former.
3. Headless D-C is independently attested and discourse-sensitive. It must not be silently treated as the same overt-head subtype.
4. Classifier+noun material is nominal and can occupy different clause roles. The NP construction must remain role-neutral.
5. Demonstratives also occur in non-deictic and structurally ambiguous corpus examples. Those examples cannot be forced into the narrow deictic overt-head subtype without exact analysis.
