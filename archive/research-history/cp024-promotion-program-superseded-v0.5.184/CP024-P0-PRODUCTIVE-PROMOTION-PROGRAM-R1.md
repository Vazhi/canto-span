# CP024 productive-promotion program R1

Date: 2026-07-20  
Accepted runtime: **v0.5.182**  
Runtime behavior changed: **no**

## Objective

Resolve every active grammar claim to a defensible terminal state while maximizing the number of narrow language constructions that qualify as `supported_productive`.

`Supported_productive` is an evidence conclusion, not a quota. The program must not promote a label merely because the label exists, the parser recognizes examples, regression tests pass, or several sources discuss a broader domain.

## Terminal-state contract

Every active public label must eventually reach one of these outcomes:

1. **`supported_productive`** — a narrow productive Cantonese construction with exact-family authority, independent natural evidence, boundaries, competing-analysis control, prospective held-out success, and learner-facing render acceptance.
2. **supported bounded lexicalized** — a conventional expression or closed family for which productivity is not claimed.
3. **internal-only non-licensing** — a parser span, graph edge, wrapper, or diagnostic that cannot independently license Cantonese behavior.
4. **retired, merged, or decomposed** — an umbrella, duplicate, obsolete label, or misanalysis replaced by narrower claims.
5. **quarantined** — a temporary state while a material evidence conflict or source gap remains unresolved.

The endpoint is therefore not “179 labels all marked productive.” It is “every retained productive language construction supported; every nonproductive or internal label explicitly bounded so it cannot masquerade as grammar.”

## Two-track execution

### Track A — promotion throughput

Select the narrowest high-readiness language construction that can close a complete evidence and evaluation packet with low architectural risk. Current target: **`OvertHeadDemonstrativeClassifierNP`**.

### Track B — dependency unblocking

Remove foundations that prevent multiple promotions. Current target: **valency and argument-structure decomposition**, including retirement of `ProductiveVO`, `TransitiveVP`, and `IntransitiveVP` as public productivity claims.

A Track B change may improve architecture without itself adding a `supported_productive` label. Such work is required when direct promotion would otherwise certify a misleading umbrella.

## Promotion order

1. narrow constructions already close to exact source mapping and bounded parser behavior;
2. nominal foundations needed by several families;
3. aspect and result families with lexical-selection controls;
4. clause relations and argument structure after internal wrappers are separated;
5. high-ambiguity discourse, ellipsis, and stance families after context and antecedent architecture is mature.

Priority may change when a dependency blocks several higher-ranked families.

## Non-negotiable promotion gates

- exact source-originated claim and explicit forbidden inferences;
- at least three independent natural attestations of the exact family by default, with locators and independence adjudication;
- controlled variation across more than one lexical item where productivity is claimed;
- negative boundaries and competing analyses;
- visible packet frozen before implementation;
- independently sealed prospective held-out custody outside the recovery tree;
- smallest authorized implementation;
- focused, semantic, aggregate, and package validation;
- learner-facing Obsidian render review;
- native-conflict escalation when the exact family is disputed.

No gate is waived to increase the status count.

## Current program decision

No additional promotion is authorized at CP024-P0-R1. The program structure, label dispositions, first throughput packet, and dependency decomposition are frozen. `OvertHeadDemonstrativeClassifierNP` may proceed to implementation only after exact natural-attestation and independent-custody gates close.
