## Material conclusions

1. Cantonese supports both restricted bare modification and `嘅`-marked modification, but their distribution and interpretation are not interchangeable.
2. `嘅` follows multiple modifier types and does not itself determine possession, association, relativization, aboutness, or complementation.
3. `啲 + noun` is source-linked as a plural-classifier nominal expression; one fixed English determiner gloss and NP-internal object role are not justified.
4. Cantonese has classifier relatives and `嘅` relatives. Their overt forms and analyses differ, so `RelativeClauseNP` must split and remain theory-neutral about gaps.
5. Headless modifier-`嘅` is referential and discourse-dependent, but it does not authorize a fabricated noun token.
6. `StativeNominalComplement` conflates headless nominalization, headed nominal modification, and potentially final/predicative `嘅`; it must split and be relabeled.
