# SV-RQ-009–012 — First research round

## Scope

This round covers the four remaining survey-derived questions not included in the `用嚟`/purposive cluster and not already covered by the deeper `SV-RQ-008` package:

- `V咗O` plus path, goal, or result-location material;
- clause-final `V咗喇`;
- `NP 用嚟做咩㗎`;
- coordination inside the purpose VP.

## SV-RQ-009 — `V咗O` followed by goal, path, or location material

### Survey signal

Both ordinary `V咗O` and matched extensions with path or location material received 100% natural judgments in the complete sample.

### Direct source findings

Kwan's corpus-and-judgment study distinguishes preverbal and postverbal locative PPs in Cantonese.

The central contrast is:

- preverbal locative PP: event location;
- postverbal locative PP: result or goal location of an affected participant.

Kwan gives the postverbal template:

```text
[V NP PP]
```

with an overt-object example:

```text
佢擺咗件衫喺衣櫃。
```

This directly supports a structure in which:

- `擺咗件衫` contains the perfective placement event and object;
- `喺衣櫃` identifies the object's resulting location.

Kataoka independently describes postverbal locative phrases with displacement, posture, appearance, and placement verbs as arguments denoting the resulting location of the object or subject.

Yiu's study of Cantonese directionals shows that directional complements may introduce path or location objects and that aspect placement depends on the directional structure.

### Interim decision

The survey's near-ceiling extension pattern is not evidence for one unrestricted `V咗O + anything` construction.

At least three larger structures must remain distinct:

1. placement predicate + object + result-location PP;
2. motion predicate + goal/location;
3. motion or placement predicate + directional/path complement.

`AB30` may remain a local overt-object perfective constituent where independently justified, but the full clause requires separate motion, placement, directional, or locative analysis.

### Not established

- that all verbs license postobject goals;
- that every postobject PP has result semantics;
- that directional and `喺`-PP extensions share constituency;
- that the object is always the located figure;
- that path, goal, and final location form one parser family.

### Next round

Cross:

- motion versus placement verbs;
- animate versus inanimate themes;
- `喺` result locations versus directional complements;
- path versus goal objects;
- preverbal versus postverbal locatives;
- perfective versus nonperfective clauses.

## SV-RQ-010 — Clause-final `V咗喇` versus overt-object perfective

### Survey signal

Clause-final `V咗喇` received 96% natural judgments; overt-object `V咗O` received 100%.

### Direct source findings

Fan and Chan analyze `咗` as a Hong Kong Cantonese perfective marker attached within the verbal domain.

Yiu's aspectual study of final particles treats `喇` as associated with inchoative and change-of-state meaning rather than as a second copy of verbal perfective aspect.

Research on Cantonese-English bilingual development likewise distinguishes:

- `咗`: event completion or perfective viewpoint;
- `喇`: current relevance, inchoativity, or change of state.

The sequence `V咗 ... 喇` can therefore combine verbal perfective marking with a clause-level change-of-state/current-relevance contribution.

### Interim decision

`V咗喇` should not be analyzed merely as `V咗O` with an omitted object.

At minimum, three variables are independent:

1. whether the verb has an overt object;
2. whether verbal perfective `咗` is present;
3. whether clause-final `喇` presents a newly relevant or changed state.

Object omission may occur, but it is not the defining property of the particle combination.

### Next round

Use contexts that separate:

- completed past report without present relevance;
- newly completed event;
- state transition;
- inchoative ongoing event;
- recoverable versus nonrecoverable object;
- `V咗`, `V喇`, and `V咗喇`.

## SV-RQ-011 — Use-function wh-question `NP 用嚟做咩㗎`

### Survey signal

The purpose-question condition received 96% natural judgments.

### Direct source findings

The lexical core `用嚟` asks about or states an object's function. `做咩` supplies a wh-expression over the purpose/action.

Recent direct work on Cantonese `gaa3` identifies exclamative, declarative, and interrogative constructions and distinguishes information-seeking, warning, and quick-answer-demanding contexts.

Kwok's particle analysis characterizes `gaa3` in declaratives as linked to shared or presupposed knowledge and distinguishes related particle forms used to question or correct assumptions.

Therefore, final `㗎` in:

```text
NP 用嚟做咩㗎？
```

may contribute speaker stance, expectation of an accountable answer, or a presupposition that the object has a relevant function. It is not safely treated as a semantically empty generic question marker.

### Interim decision

The construction is best decomposed provisionally into:

1. a use-function predicate;
2. a purpose wh-expression;
3. an interrogative or stance-bearing final particle.

No dedicated `用嚟做咩㗎` construction is justified after one round.

### Next round

Compare:

- `用嚟做咩？`
- `用嚟做咩㗎？`
- `用嚟做乜？`
- `有咩用？`
- `攞嚟做咩？`

under:

- neutral information request;
- challenge;
- reminder;
- correction;
- visible unfamiliar object;
- known object used unexpectedly.

## SV-RQ-012 — Shared-resource coordination under `用嚟`

### Survey signal

Coordinated purpose VPs received 96% natural judgments.

### Source findings

Direct Cantonese grammar recognizes ordinary conjunction and serial-verb structures. No source located in this first pass identifies coordinated purpose VPs under `用嚟` as a special construction.

The survey items are compatible with ordinary coordination inside the function/purpose complement:

```text
resource + 用嚟 + [VP1 同 VP2]
```

The shared resource and implicit user are recoverable for both conjuncts when the two purposes are semantically compatible.

### Interim decision

The default analysis is ordinary VP coordination under a use-function predicate.

A separate construction would require evidence for behavior not predicted by ordinary coordination, such as:

- special restrictions on shared objects or users;
- asymmetric scope;
- mandatory semantic compatibility;
- unusual particle placement;
- inability to coordinate independently valid VPs.

### Next round

Test:

- same versus different implicit objects;
- same versus different users;
- compatible versus incompatible functions;
- VP coordination versus clause coordination;
- `同`, `同埋`, and juxtaposition;
- aspect and negation scoped over one or both conjuncts.
