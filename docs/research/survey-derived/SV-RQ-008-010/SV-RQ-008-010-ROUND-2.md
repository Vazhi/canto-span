# SV-RQ-008–SV-RQ-010 — second research round

## Scope

This round deepens three linked questions:

1. the distribution of `V完咗O`;
2. the structure of `V咗O` followed by goal, path, directional, or result-location material;
3. the contribution of sentence-final `喇` in `V咗…喇`.

The round distinguishes:

- structural availability;
- lexical and event-structural distribution;
- discourse licensing;
- regional distribution;
- corpus occurrence;
- parser identity.

No source, attestation, or survey result in this package independently authorizes a parser or construction-status change.

## SV-RQ-008 — `V完咗O`

### 1. Structural compatibility is no longer the open question

Yip 2025 defines Cantonese inner-aspect elements partly by their ability to precede outer perfective `咗`. `完` is one of the identified inner-aspect elements. The proposed order is:

```text
verbal root < resultative complement < inner aspect < outer aspect
```

Sio and Bond's 2025 HPSG analysis independently models inner and outer aspect as different grammatical components. Inner aspect is compatible with a restricted subset of outer aspect, including perfective and experiential marking, but not progressive, continuous, or delimitative outer aspect.

Together, these analyses support:

```text
[V + 完] + 咗 + object
```

as a structurally available composition. They do not imply that every lexical realization is equally frequent or equally acceptable without context.

### 2. Exact direct examples remain narrow

Direct scholarly examples include:

```text
食完咗飯好耐嘍
佢食完咗啲飯
```

The first includes an elapsed-time expression and a final particle. The second supplies a plain overt-object example.

A low-grade public Hong Kong attestation also contains:

```text
睇完咗好耐
```

This does not establish productivity, but it independently reinforces a possible association between layered completion and retrospective or elapsed-time anchoring.

### 3. The survey contrast may reflect felicity, not a verb-class ban

The pilot produced higher acceptance for `睇完咗` and `寫完咗` than for `食完咗` and `搬完咗`. The direct literature nevertheless supplies `食完咗`, so the low survey result for one eating item cannot support a categorical consumption-verb restriction.

The current hypothesis order is therefore:

1. **discourse anchoring** — retrospective, elapsed-time, contrastive, or result-state contexts may improve the layered form;
2. **object and event structure** — definiteness, incremental theme, affectedness, and culmination may condition ratings;
3. **lexical frequency** — individual verb-object combinations may differ in conventionality;
4. **regional and speaker variation** — Hong Kong, Guangzhou, Macau, and other Yue varieties may not align;
5. **prescriptive or register pressure** — some speakers may reject apparent redundancy despite recognizing the structure.

No verb class is currently established as categorically licensing or prohibiting `完咗`.

### 4. Regional scope must remain narrow

Kwok, Chin, and Tsou's field study across nine Yue dialects identifies perfective markers and their VP position as a domain of substantial grammatical diversity. Therefore:

- Hong Kong Cantonese evidence cannot be generalized to Yue as a whole;
- a Hong Kong-heavy survey cannot establish Guangzhou or Macau distribution;
- script preference must not be used as a proxy for regional grammar.

### 5. Revised classification

`SV-RQ-008` is now:

> structurally supported; directly attested; distributionally unresolved.

The default analysis remains composition of inner completive `完` and outer perfective `咗`. A dedicated construction identity would require noncompositional restrictions or meanings not predicted by the two aspect layers.

## SV-RQ-009 — postobject result, goal, path, and direction

### 1. The survey condition contained more than one structure

The surface template:

```text
V咗 O X
```

does not identify the relation contributed by `X`.

Direct Cantonese research requires at least these distinctions:

1. `喺`-PP denoting the resulting location of the affected object;
2. intransitive motion plus a goal or final location;
3. directional verb or complement expressing path;
4. placement or displacement predicate selecting a result location;
5. preverbal event-location PP, which is not interchangeable with a postverbal result PP.

### 2. Result-location `喺` phrases

Kwan's corpus-and-judgment study gives:

```text
佢擺咗件衫喺衣櫃。
佢掉咗本書喺地下。
```

The postverbal `喺` phrase denotes where the affected participant ends up after the event. Kwan contrasts this with preverbal `喺` phrases that locate the event itself.

Kataoka likewise identifies displacement, posture, appearance, and placement verbs as major classes allowing postverbal locatives. With transitive predicates, the postverbal phrase locates the object as a result of the event.

### 3. Object type may affect structure

Sybesma's locative-resultative analysis distinguishes structures involving definite and indefinite objects. This means a parser or survey should not assume that:

```text
V + definite object + locative PP
```

and:

```text
V + indefinite object + locative PP
```

have identical syntax merely because both are acceptable.

Object properties to record include:

- definiteness;
- classifier form;
- quantization;
- affectedness;
- whether the object is the located figure;
- whether the object is introduced, moved, placed, or merely associated with the location.

### 4. Directional complements remain separate

Yiu's work shows that Cantonese uses both directional verbs and directional complements to encode path. Directional complements in Cantonese are less tightly fused with the preceding verb than their Mandarin counterparts.

Consequently:

- a directional complement is not automatically a locative PP;
- a goal NP is not automatically a `喺`-PP;
- motion and placement predicates require separate attachment analyses;
- aspect placement must be evaluated inside each directional structure.

### 5. Revised classification

`SV-RQ-009` remains direct-core but requires an explicit split:

| Profile | Provisional analysis |
|---|---|
| `V咗 O 喺 Location` with placement/displacement verb | result-location argument |
| motion verb + goal/location | motion-goal structure |
| lexical verb + directional complement | directional complex or serial structure |
| `喺 Location V咗 O` | event-location adjunct |
| postobject path phrase | path structure requiring independent evidence |

The survey's unanimous ratings support the tested examples only. They do not establish unrestricted postobject extension.

## SV-RQ-010 — `咗…喇`

### 1. Two grammatical layers are independently supported

Fan and Chan analyze preverbal-object `咗` as verbal perfective marking. Yiu analyzes final `喇` as marking the anticipation or coming-about of a new situation, change of state, or both.

Research on Cantonese-English bilingual development supplies the explicit combination:

```text
我返咗屋企喇。
```

with the interpretation that the completed return has current relevance and contrasts with a prior state in which the speaker had not returned.

This supports:

```text
[event completed with 咗] + [new/currently relevant state with 喇]
```

### 2. `喇` is not dependent on object omission

Final `喇` can occur with:

- overt objects;
- intransitive predicates;
- stative or inchoative predicates;
- future or imminent changes;
- completed events with current relevance.

Therefore, the absence of an overt object in a short `V咗喇` string is an independent discourse fact. It does not define the particle combination.

### 3. Current relevance predicts continuation restrictions

A `咗…喇` clause presents a result or changed state as relevant at the reference time. A following clause that immediately cancels that state may require a shifted reference time or additional explanation.

This suggests a useful discourse diagnostic:

- continuation compatible with the resulting/current state;
- continuation that reverses it later;
- continuation that contradicts it at the same reference time;
- explicit past-time narration with no present relevance.

This diagnostic should be tested rather than encoded as a categorical parser constraint.

### 4. Required comparison set

The next controlled study should distinguish:

1. `V O`
2. `V咗 O`
3. `V O 喇`
4. `V咗 O 喇`
5. `V咗 喇` with recoverable object
6. `V咗 喇` without a recoverable object

Contexts should cross:

- simple completed past report;
- newly completed event;
- newly true state;
- imminent transition;
- contrary-to-expectation result;
- current-relevance cancellation.

### 5. Revised classification

`SV-RQ-010` is:

> direct-core composition of verbal perfective and clause-level change/current relevance; discourse boundary unresolved.

No special construction defined by object omission is supported.

## Cross-question implications

### Aspect and result are layered

The three questions converge on a layered analysis:

- inner completion may combine with outer perfective;
- outer perfective may occur inside a larger result-location or directional clause;
- clause-final `喇` may add a discourse-level change or current-relevance meaning.

Surface adjacency is therefore insufficient for deciding construction identity.

### Required evidence sequence

Before implementation decisions:

1. direct-source boundary;
2. corpus occurrence with complete context;
3. lexical and object classification;
4. matched native-speaker judgments;
5. regional stratification;
6. collision analysis against existing identities;
7. held-out examples.

## Research-only conclusion

The second round narrows the questions without closing them:

- `V完咗O` is a supported aspect stack whose felicity and distribution require controlled study;
- postobject extensions split into result-location, motion-goal, path, and directional profiles;
- `咗…喇` combines event completion with a new or currently relevant state and is not reducible to object ellipsis.
