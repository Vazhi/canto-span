# SV-RQ-001–007 — Use-function and purposive `嚟` cluster

## Scope

This is the first independent-source round for the seven survey-derived questions involving:

- `NP 用嚟 VP`;
- resource-like initial NPs;
- overt-user strings;
- `係用嚟 ... (嘅)`;
- `用咗嚟 VP`;
- `用嚟 V咗O`;
- `V咗 NP 嚟 VP`;
- context-recovered `用嚟 VP` fragments.

The private survey provides the questions and response patterns. It does not itself establish constituency, productivity, or parser boundaries.

## Evidence discipline

The direct Cantonese evidence is uneven.

Three evidence layers are kept separate:

1. **Direct Cantonese construction evidence**
   - Cantonese purpose serial-verb descriptions;
   - research on purposive `嚟`;
   - Hong Kong Cantonese perfective `咗`;
   - Cantonese topicalization and omitted arguments;
   - Cantonese lexicography for `用嚟`.

2. **Reference-grammar support**
   - Matthews and Yip's Cantonese grammar materials for topic prominence, hanging topics, serial verbs, omitted pronouns, `係`, aspect, and particles.

3. **Comparative Chinese hypotheses**
   - Mandarin `VP-來-VP`, fragment, and null-argument analyses.
   - These may suggest diagnostics but may not be transferred into Cantonese conclusions.

## Shared source findings

### A. `用嚟` directly introduces an object's function

The Cantonese lexical entry in 粵典 defines `用嚟` as:

> 帶出物件嘅用途

Its example uses a money NP whose function is paying rent:

```text
啲錢用嚟交租㗎。
```

This is direct lexical evidence for a relation between a resource-like NP and a function/purpose VP. It does not define a closed semantic inventory and does not establish that every initial NP is the grammatical subject.

### B. Purposive `嚟` is a grammaticalized Cantonese particle

Sze's study of directional-particle grammaticalization states that both `嚟` and `去` developed purposive uses, with `去` more restricted than `嚟`. This supports separating purposive `嚟` from directional `嚟`.

Wong, Cheung, Lo, and Wan describe Cantonese purpose serial-verb constructions as structures where the first verb denotes an action and the following verb or verbs express that action's purpose. Their Cantonese example includes:

```text
買本書 (嚟) 睇吓。
```

They separately note that sequential serial constructions often mark completion of the first action with perfective `咗`.

Together, these sources directly support a Cantonese family in which an initial action or resource-acquisition event is followed by purposive `嚟 + VP`.

### C. Cantonese independently permits topic–comment and double-subject strings

Matthews and Yip distinguish:

- object topicalization;
- hanging topics and double subjects;
- secondary topicalization;
- topic chains.

Their hanging-topic example has an initial NP followed by an overt subject:

```text
錢，我幫你唔到嘞。
```

This establishes that the surface order `NP + subject + predicate` is independently available in Cantonese. It does not prove that every `Resource NP + Agent + 用嚟 VP` token is derived by topicalization, nor whether the initial NP is moved or base-generated.

### D. Cantonese permits discourse-recovered omitted arguments

Matthews and Yip explicitly document omitted pronouns and topic chains. This establishes that a missing overt participant in a context-supported clause is not exceptional in Cantonese.

It does not decide between:

- null pronoun;
- topic-bound variable;
- argument ellipsis;
- clausal ellipsis;
- answer-fragment derivation.

Direct Cantonese diagnostics are still required.

### E. Perfective `咗` must be analyzed at its actual predicate position

Fan and Chan's study of Hong Kong Cantonese `咗` finds that in a complex VP, `咗` attaches to the first predicate of the relevant construction. This is important for distinguishing:

```text
用咗嚟 VP
```

from:

```text
用嚟 V咗O
```

The two strings place perfective marking in different event domains. The source does not analyze these exact `用嚟` strings, so this is a structural diagnostic rather than direct attestation.

## Question-by-question first-round decisions

## SV-RQ-001 — Resource-class licensing in `NP 用嚟 VP`

### Survey signal

Complete-sample naturalness:

- resource/instrument: 79%;
- location: 92%;
- mixed nonhuman roles: 67%;
- person: 29%.

### Source-linked interpretation

The direct lexical description supports an object-function relation. It is therefore unsafe to broaden the pattern into unrestricted `NP + 用嚟 + VP`.

The source round supports the following provisional core:

```text
resource-like NP + 用嚟 + function/purpose VP
```

The category **resource-like** is not yet formally delimited. The survey suggests internal gradience:

- instruments and documents are straightforward resources;
- money is directly lexically attested;
- rooms and locations may be construed as facilities;
- methods, opportunities, and bounded time intervals show greater lexical and contextual sensitivity;
- human names without another resource are poor candidates for the same role.

### Interim decision

- Retain a resource/function research core.
- Do not license arbitrary topical NPs through this construction.
- Do not infer that all acceptable initial NPs share one syntactic relation.
- Treat location, money, document, method, opportunity, and time-interval subclasses as separate follow-up cells.

### Next round

Test each semantic class with:

1. no overt user;
2. overt user;
3. active `Agent 用 NP 嚟 VP`;
4. explicit function noun paraphrase;
5. forced role interpretation.

## SV-RQ-002 — Overt-user resource-fronting

### Survey signal

`Resource NP + Agent + 用嚟 + VP` received 100% natural judgments in the complete sample.

### Source-linked interpretation

Cantonese independently permits hanging topics followed by overt subjects. The survey string is therefore compatible with a topic–comment analysis:

```text
Resource NP, Agent 用嚟 VP
```

However, the available sources do not establish:

- whether there is movement;
- whether a null object is bound by the initial NP;
- whether the initial NP is a hanging topic or an internal topic;
- whether `用嚟` itself licenses the fronting;
- whether prosody or a topic particle is required in particular contexts.

### Interim decision

The overt-user pattern is a real surface profile requiring independent representation in research, but **not yet a separate language-construction identity**.

The preferred first hypothesis is ordinary Cantonese topic–comment structure combined with a use-function predicate. This remains defeasible.

### Diagnostics for the next round

- pause and prosodic-boundary judgments;
- topic-particle compatibility;
- resumptive-pronoun compatibility;
- island sensitivity;
- contrastive versus neutral contexts;
- definite versus indefinite initial resources;
- comparison with non-`用嚟` predicates.

## SV-RQ-003 — Copular `NP 係用嚟 VP (嘅)`

### Survey signal

`NP 係用嚟 VP` received 92% natural judgments, but several respondents added final `嘅`.

### Source-linked interpretation

Direct Cantonese grammar distinguishes `係` as a copula with nominal predicates. Other scholarship notes that `係` may function as an optional focus marker outside obligatory nominal-predicate contexts.

The available direct sources do not analyze `係用嚟 VP (嘅)` as a dedicated construction. The survey repairs are compatible with several clause-level packages:

- identification of conventional function;
- contrastive or corrective focus;
- explanatory assertion;
- an `係 ... 嘅` focus/explanatory frame;
- ordinary copular predication over a nominalized function expression.

### Interim decision

Treat the pattern as a **clause-level compositional audit**, not a new use-function construction.

The final `嘅` preference cannot be encoded as obligatory from the current evidence.

### Next round

Use matched discourse contexts for:

1. `NP 用嚟 VP`;
2. `NP 係用嚟 VP`;
3. `NP 係用嚟 VP 嘅`;
4. `NP 係 [用嚟 VP] 嘅` under explicit correction;
5. negated and contrastive variants.

Collect interpretation and information-structure judgments separately from naturalness.

## SV-RQ-004 — Actual-use `NP 用咗嚟 VP`

### Survey signal

`用咗嚟 VP` received 79% natural and 8% unnatural judgments.

### Source-linked interpretation

Purposive `嚟` is independently supported, and Hong Kong Cantonese `咗` may attach to the first predicate in a complex VP. These two facts make the following compositional analysis structurally plausible:

```text
[用咗 resource] + 嚟 + purpose VP
```

or, in a resource-initial alternation:

```text
resource + [用咗] + 嚟 + purpose VP
```

No source in this first pass directly analyzes the exact string `用咗嚟 VP`, so the survey remains the principal exact-form signal.

### Interim decision

- Reject a categorical prohibition on `用咗嚟 VP`.
- Treat it provisionally as perfective actual use plus purposive `嚟`.
- Do not create a dedicated construction identity unless corpus and contrast work reveals non-compositional restrictions.
- Keep generic function `用嚟 VP` and episodic actual use analytically distinct.

### Next round

Search natural corpora for exact `用咗嚟` tokens and classify:

- resource type;
- overt or implicit agent;
- temporal anchoring;
- discourse contrast;
- final particles;
- passive or active syntax;
- whether the purpose event occurred.

## SV-RQ-005 — Perfective inside the purpose complement

### Survey signal

`NP 用嚟 V咗O` received:

- natural: 63%;
- uncertain: 4%;
- unnatural: 33%.

### Source-linked interpretation

The pattern differs structurally from `用咗嚟 VP` because perfective marking falls inside the purpose VP rather than on `用`.

If `用嚟` states conventional function, a completed event in its complement can create a semantic mismatch unless the sentence is coerced into an episodic or explanatory context. The first source round does not prove such a restriction, but the survey split is consistent with it.

Mandarin work on instrument-purpose `VP-來-VP` proposes one-event and multi-event subtypes. This is useful only as a comparative hypothesis; it is not Cantonese evidence.

### Interim decision

- Do not generalize unrestricted `用嚟 V咗O`.
- Do not classify it as categorically ill-formed.
- Treat acceptance as potentially dependent on generic versus episodic interpretation, discourse accommodation, and lexical frame.
- Keep the perfective complement independently analyzable as `AB30`-type material rather than absorbing it into the use-function identity.

### Next round

Cross the same lexical sets across:

1. `用嚟 VO`;
2. `用咗嚟 VO`;
3. `用嚟 V咗O`;
4. `用咗嚟 V咗O`;

under:

- generic-function context;
- one-time actual-use context;
- retrospective explanation;
- corrective focus.

## SV-RQ-006 — Perfective acquisition plus purposive `嚟`

### Survey signal

`Agent V咗 NP 嚟 VP` received 88% natural judgments. The weak lexical item used `空咗時間`; respondents preferred `預留咗` or another reservation verb.

### Direct source support

The purpose-serial description and example `買本書 (嚟) 睇吓` directly support the constructional family. The separate observation that sequential serial constructions often perfectivize the first action supports forms in which acquisition or preparation is completed before the purpose event.

Sze's grammaticalization study independently supports purposive `嚟`.

### Interim decision

The broad family is directly supported:

```text
Agent + action/acquisition VP + (嚟) + purpose VP
```

The unresolved issue is lexical selection in the first VP, not existence of the purpose relation.

Provisional first-VP subclasses:

- acquisition: `買`, `攞`, `借`;
- creation/preparation;
- allocation/reservation: `留`, `預留`;
- transfer or possession change;
- motion;
- stative or weakly agentive predicates, expected to be more restricted.

### Next round

Build a verb-class matrix controlling the same resource and purpose VP. Test:

- optionality of `嚟`;
- perfective versus bare first VP;
- agent control;
- event sequence;
- whether VP1 creates, acquires, selects, reserves, or merely describes the resource.

## SV-RQ-007 — Context-recovered `用嚟 VP` fragment

### Survey signal

The fragment condition received:

- natural: 75%;
- uncertain: 21%;
- unnatural: 4%.

The dominant reason was need for prior context.

### Source-linked interpretation

Cantonese grammar independently permits omitted pronouns and topic chains. Therefore a surface string beginning with `用嚟` can be licensed through discourse recovery without requiring a special fragment construction.

However, direct Cantonese sources in this pass do not distinguish among:

- ordinary null argument;
- topic-bound null object;
- full-clause ellipsis;
- answer fragment;
- lexicalized predicate with implicit resource.

Mandarin fragment and null-object studies provide diagnostics but not transferable conclusions.

### Interim decision

- Recognize a context-dependent surface profile.
- Do not assume a context-free clause.
- Do not choose between null argument and clausal ellipsis from naturalness ratings alone.
- Do not merge this automatically with generic `FragmentAnswer` or a broad omitted-complement wrapper.

### Next round

Use explicit contexts:

- `呢樣嘢有咩用？`
- `你攞佢做咩？`
- visible-object context;
- contrastive set of resources;
- no antecedent.

Test:

- sloppy versus strict readings;
- quantificational antecedents;
- correction contexts;
- answer versus continuation;
- overt demonstrative or pronoun substitution;
- prosodic completeness.

## Cluster-level collision decision

The first source round supports a conservative decomposition:

1. **Use-function relation**
   - resource-like NP related to a function/purpose VP.

2. **Ordinary Cantonese topic–comment structure**
   - may produce resource-first, overt-user strings.

3. **Purposive `嚟`**
   - independently grammaticalized and available after action/resource-acquisition VPs.

4. **Perfective `咗`**
   - may scope over the first action or occur inside the purpose VP, producing different event structures.

5. **Discourse recovery**
   - may omit a recoverable resource or larger clause material.

No new parser matcher, construction identity, status change, promotion, or release action is authorized.
