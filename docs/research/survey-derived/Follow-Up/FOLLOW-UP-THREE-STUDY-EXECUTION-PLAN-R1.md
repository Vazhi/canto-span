# Three-study follow-up execution plan R1

## Purpose

Resolve the three highest-priority survey-derived boundaries without turning the pilot results into expected-answer cues:

1. perfective marking inside a use-function complement: `用嚟 V咗O`;
2. context and prosody licensing for bare `用嚟 VP`;
3. lexical, discourse, and regional distribution of `V完咗O`.

The three studies must be deployed as separate short instruments. Combining them would create avoidable fatigue, mix incompatible response tasks, and obscure which manipulation caused a rating difference.

## Shared safeguards

- Use adult native or childhood Cantonese speakers under one pooled inclusion protocol.
- Record childhood variety, current-use variety, and script preference separately.
- Do not infer regional variety from Traditional or Simplified script selection.
- Provide Traditional and Simplified versions of identical Cantonese content.
- Preserve Cantonese-specific characters such as `嘅`, `咗`, `嚟`, `喺`, `唔`, `㗎`, and `喇` in both script versions.
- Randomize target items and fillers, with no more than two critical items consecutively.
- Present one condition from each lexical set to any one respondent.
- Keep exact stimulus version, list, script, and randomization order.
- Treat corrections as hypothesis-generating evidence, not authoritative analyses.
- Do not expose parser labels, research IDs, expected rankings, or implementation consequences.
- Freeze and hash each instrument before collection.

## Study 1 — context and interpretation of `用嚟 V咗O`

### Neutral question

Under which discourse conditions does a perfective event following `用嚟` receive a function, actual-use, completed-event, or unacceptable interpretation?

### Core form contrast

Cross identical lexical frames across:

1. `NP 用嚟 VO`
2. `NP 用嚟 V咗O`
3. `NP 用咗嚟 VO`
4. `NP 用咗嚟 V咗O`

No lexical material may change within a matched set other than the target morphology.

### Contexts

1. **generic function** — asks what the resource is normally or conventionally for;
2. **completed actual use** — asks what the resource was actually used for in a completed episode;
3. **intended-versus-actual contrast** — states an intended purpose and asks what actually happened;
4. **no context** — presents the sentence alone.

### Lexical-frame requirements

Use common, semantically plausible frames from several resource classes:

- money or budget;
- bounded time;
- room or bounded location;
- physical instrument;
- document, form, or digital tool;
- material or container.

Avoid items whose lexical wording was independently criticized in the pilot. Do not use `空咗時間`. Keep object definiteness and classifier form constant inside each set.

### Response sequence

For every target item:

1. five-point naturalness rating;
2. forced interpretation:
   - conventional or intended function;
   - actual completed use;
   - both readings are available;
   - understandable but the context does not fit;
   - no coherent reading;
3. optional reason category;
4. optional correction.

The interpretation task must precede correction so that correction behavior does not replace evidence about the respondent's original reading.

### Recommended size

- 8 lexical sets;
- 4 contexts;
- 4 counterbalanced lists;
- 8 target judgments per respondent;
- 8–12 fillers;
- target duration: 6–9 minutes.

### Primary analysis

Use ordinal naturalness as the primary outcome. Model form, context, resource class, and their interactions, with respondent and lexical item as random effects when sample size permits. Analyze forced interpretation separately rather than treating it as a reason code.

### Decision boundary

The study may establish context-sensitive distribution or a preferred compositional analysis. It cannot by itself authorize unrestricted productivity, a parser identity, or a rejection rule.

## Study 2 — context and prosody of bare `用嚟 VP`

### Neutral question

When a surface string begins with `用嚟 VP`, what discourse antecedent and prosodic realization allow listeners to recover the missing resource?

### Written context conditions

Use the same target string after:

1. an explicit function question;
2. a previous sentence introducing the resource;
3. a visible or jointly attended referent described in text or image;
4. a contrastive correction;
5. no antecedent.

Include an overt-resource control such as `呢個用嚟 VP` for every lexical frame.

### Prosody stage

Run audio as a separate stage or separate randomized block. Do not rely on punctuation as a substitute for prosody.

For each selected target, record at least:

1. an answer-fragment realization linked to a preceding question;
2. an independent-statement realization;
3. an overt-resource control.

Use more than one native speaker or counterbalance speaker across conditions so that one voice is not confounded with one prosody. Keep segmental wording identical. Record and preserve the audio take, speaker variety, and item-to-list mapping.

### Response sequence

1. naturalness;
2. antecedent identification:
   - explicit resource in the context;
   - visible or jointly attended object;
   - generic or unspecified resource;
   - no recoverable antecedent;
3. confidence in antecedent choice;
4. whether the utterance sounds like an answer, a complete statement, or an incomplete fragment;
5. optional correction.

### Recommended size

Written stage:

- 6 lexical frames;
- 5 context conditions;
- one target condition per lexical set;
- 6 target judgments plus 6–10 fillers;
- target duration: 5–7 minutes.

Audio stage:

- 6 lexical frames;
- 3 realizations;
- 6 target judgments plus fillers;
- target duration: 5–7 minutes.

### Primary analysis

Separate naturalness from antecedent recovery. A sentence may be structurally interpretable but pragmatically infelicitous without a salient resource. Prosody effects must be analyzed independently from written-context effects.

### Decision boundary

Possible outcomes include answer fragment, null-resource clause, topic-bound omission, ellipsis, or more than one licensed analysis. The study should not force all uses into one derivation.

## Study 3 — regional matched-form replication of `V完咗O`

### Neutral question

How do aspectual form, lexical class, discourse context, object properties, and Cantonese variety affect judgments of completion expressions containing `完` and `咗`?

### Core forms

For every lexical set compare:

1. `V完O`
2. `V完O喇`
3. `V咗O`
4. `V完咗O`

A later extension may test `V完咗O喇`, but it should not increase the first replication beyond a manageable four-form design.

### Lexical classes

Use at least two lexical frames from each class:

- reading, viewing, or information consumption;
- writing, drawing, or creation;
- eating or drinking;
- caused motion or transfer;
- conventional task completion;
- preparation, repair, or assembly.

Keep subject, verb, object, classifier, definiteness, and context constant within each matched set.

### Contexts

1. **current-result question** — asks whether the task is now complete;
2. **elapsed-time retrospective narrative** — the event ended earlier and a later state is under discussion.

Do not mix additional pragmatic contrasts into the first replication.

### Regional recruitment

Recruit and report separately for:

- Hong Kong Cantonese;
- Guangzhou Cantonese;
- Macau Cantonese;
- other Yue backgrounds, analyzed descriptively unless adequately represented.

Planning threshold rather than evidential rule:

- minimum pilot target: 40 complete respondents per primary region, giving roughly 10 respondents per list;
- preferred replication target: 80 complete respondents per primary region, giving roughly 20 respondents per list.

A formal power analysis should replace these planning thresholds once pilot variance and item effects are available.

### Response sequence

1. five-point naturalness;
2. interpretation:
   - completed event;
   - completed event with completion especially foregrounded;
   - understandable but redundant;
   - another reading;
   - no coherent reading;
3. whether another form would be preferred;
4. optional correction;
5. optional note on regional or colloquial alternative.

### Primary analysis

Use ordinal ratings without collapsing them for the primary model. Predictors:

- form;
- lexical class;
- context;
- object type;
- childhood variety;
- current-use variety.

Pre-register comparisons of `V完咗O` against each of the three alternatives and interactions with lexical class, context, and variety.

### Decision boundary

A regional or lexical difference is not a universal grammatical rule. Scholarly attestation, corpus non-attestation, and native-speaker conflict must remain separately represented in the final evidence record.

## Recommended deployment order

1. Study 1: `用嚟 V咗O` — largest unresolved compositional boundary.
2. Study 2 written stage: bare `用嚟 VP` — determines whether prior context explains the pilot uncertainty.
3. Study 3: `V完咗O` — requires deliberate regional recruitment and therefore more preparation.
4. Study 2 audio stage — run after written contexts identify the most informative contrasts.

## Instrument-generation gate

This document is a research design, not a participant-facing survey and not a survey-generation authorization.

Before drafting SoSci items, XML, translations, audio scripts, or participant instructions, supply a separate blinded survey-generation prompt. That prompt must authorize only the neutral brief and independent linguistic sources, and must prohibit access to expected judgments, parser behavior, prior response rankings, and implementation goals.

## Repository and implementation boundary

No parser, construction identity, grammar status, fixture, test, workflow, generated runtime output, or release change is authorized by this plan.
