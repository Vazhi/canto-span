# SV-RQ-008 — Lexically and regionally conditioned `V完咗O`

## Research question

Which verb classes, endpoint structures, object properties, discourse contexts, and regional varieties permit or disfavor outer perfective `咗` after completive `完`?

The critical comparison is:

1. `V完O`
2. `V完O喇`
3. `V咗O`
4. `V完咗O`

This research unit does not ask whether the fourth pattern exists at all. The first source pass establishes that it is attested and receives a structural analysis in the scholarly literature. The unresolved problem is its distribution.

## Private survey signal

The 2026-07-24 pilot produced a polarized complete-sample result for `V完咗O`:

- natural: 13/24;
- uncertain: 0/24;
- unnatural: 11/24.

The lexical items differed:

| Item | Natural | Unnatural |
|---|---:|---:|
| `我睇完咗嗰本書` | 4/5 | 1/5 |
| `佢食完咗個三文治` | 3/9 | 6/9 |
| `阿欣寫完咗份報告` | 5/6 | 1/6 |
| `我哋搬完咗三個箱` | 1/4 | 3/4 |

These counts are hypothesis-generating only. Assignment counts were unequal, the panel was heavily Hong Kong–weighted, and each lexical item has a small sample.

## Direct source findings

### 1. Yip 2025: `完` is inner aspect below outer perfective `咗`

Yip treats completive `完` as one of fourteen postverbal inner-aspect elements. The operational definition explicitly states that an inner-aspect element can be followed by perfective `咗`. The proposed verbal template places inner aspect below outer aspect:

```text
verbal root < resultative complement < inner aspect < outer aspect < 晒
```

For `完`, the article gives the explicit sequence:

```text
食完咗飯好耐嘍
sik-jyun-zo faan hou-noi lo3
```

The example is attributed to Cheung 2007:149.

This is direct scholarly support for the order `V-完-咗-O` in at least one Hong Kong Cantonese context. It does not establish unrestricted lexical productivity or uniform speaker acceptance.

### 2. Sybesma 2013: verbal particles may intervene before `咗`

Sybesma states that elements such as `完`, treated there as an aspectual verbal particle, precede perfective `咗`. The chapter includes:

```text
佢食完咗啲飯。
keoi5 sik-jyun4-zo2 di1-faan6
```

This independently supports the structural ordering and supplies another overt-object example. The chapter's main topic is postverbal `先`, so its evidence is strong for ordering and attestation but not a full distributional analysis of `V完咗O`.

### 3. Fan and Chan 2022: `咗` placement in complex predicates

Fan and Chan analyze Hong Kong Cantonese perfective `咗` and argue that in a complex VP it attaches to the first predicate of the relevant construction. Their broader analysis supports treating `咗` as a preverbal-object outer-aspect marker rather than a sentence-final completion particle.

This source strengthens the structural distinction between:

- inner completive `完`;
- outer perfective `咗`;
- sentence-final `喇`.

It does not by itself explain why speakers vary across particular `V完咗O` strings.

## Interim research decision

### Established

1. `V完咗O` cannot be treated as categorically nonexistent or categorically ungrammatical.
2. `完` and `咗` occupy distinguishable aspectual layers in current scholarship.
3. The order `V-完-咗-O` has direct scholarly attestation.
4. `V完O`, `V咗O`, and `V完咗O` are not semantically interchangeable merely because all may describe bounded events.

### Not established

1. unrestricted productivity across verbs;
2. uniform acceptability across Hong Kong, Guangzhou, Macau, or other Yue varieties;
3. a reliable verb-class rule;
4. a reliable object-definiteness or quantization rule;
5. whether a final particle or elapsed-time phrase improves the sequence;
6. whether survey variation reflects grammar, discourse felicity, lexical frequency, prescriptive pressure, or speaker variety.

### Current hypothesis space

The survey and literature together motivate at least five nonexclusive hypotheses:

- **lexical-class conditioning:** creation and information-processing predicates may differ from consumption and caused-motion predicates;
- **event-structure conditioning:** predicates with a salient culmination may favor explicit completive plus perfective layering;
- **object conditioning:** definite, quantified, affected, or incremental-theme objects may differ;
- **discourse conditioning:** elapsed-time, current-relevance, or contrastive contexts may license the layered form more readily;
- **regional or speaker conditioning:** speakers may differ systematically by variety, age, literacy, or contact with written Mandarin.

No single hypothesis is yet preferred.

## Collision and identity implications

Relevant existing identities:

- `AA26 WanMarkedCompletionVP` should continue to represent the overt `V + 完` constituent.
- `AB30 ZoMarkedPerfectiveObjectVP` represents ordinary `V + 咗 + overt object`.
- `V完咗O` should be treated provisionally as composition of inner completive and outer perfective unless independent evidence shows construction-specific behavior.
- `V完O喇` belongs to a separate outer-clause and sentence-final-particle comparison.

No new construction identity, parser matcher, status change, or promotion is authorized by this note.

The correct current position is narrower than either categorical acceptance or categorical rejection:

> `V完咗O` is a structurally supported and directly attested Cantonese sequence whose lexical, discourse, and regional distribution remains unresolved.
