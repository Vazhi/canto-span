# PRQ2-007 — inchoative `V／A起上嚟`

## Research decision

Create one dedicated research unit for Cantonese postpredicative `起上嚟 hei2
soeng5 lai4` and its shorter related form `起嚟 hei2 lai4` when they present an
event or state as beginning, becoming manifest, or entering a contextually
relevant active phase.

The strongest supported unsplit core is:

```text
SUBJECT / TOPIC + (ONSET ADVERBIAL) + PREDICATE + 起上嚟
```

The strongest supported object-bearing core is discontinuous:

```text
SUBJECT / TOPIC + V + 起 + OBJECT + 上嚟
```

The corresponding shorter form has:

```text
SUBJECT / TOPIC + V + 起 + OBJECT + 嚟
```

Directly checked examples include:

```text
佢好突然咁喊起上嚟。
個BB突然間喊起上嚟。
佢哋又嘈起嚟。
個天好快就光起嚟。
我一嬲起上嚟就亂咁鬧人。
佢突然間學起法文嚟。
我個仔一發起脾氣上嚟好似隻老虎咁。
佢打起網球上嚟成個張德培咁。
```

Modern lexical attestations additionally include:

```text
佢哋聽到個消息之後都不禁喊起上嚟。
佢野蠻起上嚟，真係不可理喻㗎。
老闆發起脾氣上嚟好得人驚㗎。
佢有棚齙牙，笑起上嚟好明顯。
```

The supported contribution is an **onset or activation boundary**, not merely
upward movement. Depending on predicate and clause type, the construction may:

- assert that an event begins;
- present a state or property as beginning to hold;
- introduce the interval in which a characteristic behavior becomes manifest;
- establish an “once/when X starts or manifests” subordinate frame; or
- split around an overt object or the nominal member of a verb-object compound.

The evidence does not support treating every string containing `起`, `上`, and
`嚟` as one aspect suffix. Literal upward motion, caused motion, appearance or
preliminary-judgment uses such as `睇起嚟`, and discourse-linking uses such as
`講起嚟` remain separate boundaries.

This note authorizes no parser change, registry change, construction-status
change, lexicon installation, acceptance fixture, survey instrument, or master
backlog edit.

## Independent evidence base

### A. Dedicated peer-reviewed formation study

Yiu (2008) directly studies the Cantonese inchoative marker `起上嚟`.

The official HKUST research record states that:

- `起嚟` and `起上嚟` are similar aspect markers;
- both indicate that an event or state begins;
- when an object occurs, the marker is split as `起 + OBJECT + 嚟` or
  `起 + OBJECT + 上嚟`; and
- `起上嚟` developed historically through grammaticalization of a directional
  pattern involving `V + 起 + OBJECT + 上 + 嚟`.

The article traces `起嚟` to nineteenth-century materials and reports the first
`起上嚟` occurrence in materials from the 1930s. This diachronic result supports
historical formation and relatedness. It does not establish that every modern
speaker treats the two forms as interchangeable in every predicate class or
register.

### B. Official full-text study of `起嚟` and `落去`

Yiu (2016) provides direct modern examples and detailed structural contrasts.
It describes postpredicative `起嚟` as marking the beginning of an action or
change:

```text
佢哋又嘈起嚟。
個天好快就光起嚟。
```

The second example is especially important because `光` is not a physical
manner-of-motion verb. It supports onset of a state or change rather than a
literal directional path.

With an overt object, Yiu gives:

```text
佢突然間學起法文嚟。
```

The source explicitly contrasts this discontinuous profile with ordinary
monosyllabic aspect markers and with continuative `落去`.

Yiu also distinguishes literal directional uses:

```text
佢好快噉跳咗起嚟。
佢好快噉擰起本書嚟。
隻麻雀突然飛咗起嚟。
佢即刻就擰起本書嚟。
```

These involve upward movement, movement toward the speaker, or caused movement
of an object. Their existence prevents a surface-only rule that assigns aspect
to every `V起…嚟` sequence.

The chapter further argues that directional `起嚟` differs structurally from
ordinary compound directional complements. It can be split by an object and,
in the relevant directional profile, does not behave like the potential forms
available to several other compound directionals. Those observations are
important collision boundaries; they do not automatically establish every
potential or negation restriction for the aspectual use.

### C. University-hosted reference grammar

The multimedia supplement to Matthews and Yip's comprehensive grammar lists
`起上嚟` under “inchoative and continuative” and provides:

```text
佢好突然咁喊起上嚟。
```

with the interpretation that the subject suddenly started crying.

This independently supports an unsplit dynamic-event onset profile. The compact
reference entry does not establish the full object pattern, lexical inventory,
negation, or all discourse readings.

### D. Detailed contemporary grammar and workbook

Yip and Matthews (2017) state that postverbal `起上嚟` denotes the beginning of
an action. Their examples include:

```text
個BB突然間喊起上嚟。
我一嬲起上嚟就亂咁鬧人。
```

They characterize the marker as especially common in subordinate clauses,
including an `一 … 就 …` frame. This supports a construction in which the onset
or manifestation interval forms the first member of a larger dependency.

The same source explicitly describes object splitting:

```text
我個仔一發起脾氣上嚟好似隻老虎咁。
佢打起網球上嚟成個張德培咁。
```

It also supplies exercise answers with additional predicate classes:

```text
天氣轉起上嚟，好容易病。
稅局查起稅上嚟，就麻煩。
佢一黐起線上嚟，乜都做得出。
```

These exercise items are useful controlled pedagogical examples. They are not
independent natural-corpus attestations and should not alone license an
unrestricted cross-product.

### E. Modern Cantonese lexical attestations

Words.hk independently attests several profiles:

```text
佢哋聽到個消息之後都不禁喊起上嚟。
佢野蠻起上嚟，真係不可理喻㗎。
老闆發起脾氣上嚟好得人驚㗎。
佢有棚齙牙，笑起上嚟好明顯。
```

These examples broaden the observed range beyond one textbook sentence:

- triggered emotional onset after a preceding event;
- property or behavioral manifestation;
- a split verb-object compound; and
- a generic “when smiling” interpretation.

Dictionary examples demonstrate contemporary attestation. They do not provide
representative frequencies, controlled minimal contrasts, or categorical
speaker judgments.

## Narrow supported profiles

### 1. Dynamic-event onset

The clearest profile is:

```text
DYNAMIC PREDICATE + 起上嚟
```

Examples:

```text
佢好突然咁喊起上嚟。
個BB突然間喊起上嚟。
佢哋聽到個消息之後都不禁喊起上嚟。
```

The construction profiles the transition from the event not occurring to the
event occurring. An onset adverbial or causal trigger often makes this change
salient:

```text
突然
突然間
聽到個消息之後
```

The sources do not establish that such an adverbial is grammatically mandatory.
It is therefore a frequent discourse support, not a hard parser requirement.

### 2. State or property onset

Yiu directly gives:

```text
個天好快就光起嚟。
```

Words.hk supplies:

```text
佢野蠻起上嚟，真係不可理喻㗎。
```

Yip and Matthews give:

```text
天氣轉起上嚟，好容易病。
佢一黐起線上嚟，乜都做得出。
```

These examples show that the host need not be an agentive activity. A state,
property, or change predicate can enter an onset or manifestation frame.

The exact lexical boundary remains unresolved. The evidence does not justify
attaching `起上嚟` freely to every adjective, stative verb, achievement, or
already-inchoative predicate.

### 3. Subordinate onset or manifestation frame

The pattern is especially well attested in a first clause followed by a
consequence, evaluation, or characteristic behavior:

```text
我一嬲起上嚟就亂咁鬧人。
老闆發起脾氣上嚟好得人驚㗎。
佢有棚齙牙，笑起上嚟好明顯。
```

The first member can be interpreted as:

- once the state begins;
- whenever the behavior manifests;
- when the subject enters that state; or
- during the interval initiated by the marked predicate.

A future ontology may therefore need both:

1. an internal inchoative or manifestation predicate; and
2. a higher temporal, conditional, or evaluative relation.

A broad clause wrapper must not replace the internal `起上嚟` dependency.

### 4. Split transitive object

The central discontinuous pattern is:

```text
V + 起 + OBJECT + 上嚟
```

Examples:

```text
佢打起網球上嚟成個張德培咁。
稅局查起稅上嚟，就麻煩。
```

The object remains semantically selected by the lexical verb. `起` occurs
between the verb and object, while `上嚟` closes the discontinuous marker after
the object.

This structure is incompatible with an implementation that recognizes only a
contiguous string `起上嚟`.

### 5. Split verb-object compound

The same pattern applies to verb-object compounds:

```text
發脾氣
```

becomes:

```text
發起脾氣上嚟
```

Direct examples include:

```text
我個仔一發起脾氣上嚟好似隻老虎咁。
老闆發起脾氣上嚟好得人驚㗎。
```

A future analysis must preserve the lexical relation between `發` and `脾氣`
while also representing the discontinuous inchoative marker. It must not
require the pre-existing contiguous `發脾氣` span to survive unchanged.

### 6. Shorter `起嚟` variant

The dedicated formation study treats `起嚟` and `起上嚟` as similar inchoative
markers. Direct examples include:

```text
佢哋又嘈起嚟。
個天好快就光起嚟。
佢突然間學起法文嚟。
```

Lexical resources also attest:

```text
佢一句話，引到成屋人都笑起嚟。
```

This supports a related shorter family. It does not prove complete free
alternation between `起嚟` and `起上嚟` across all predicates, object types,
regions, and discourse functions.

## Semantic and discourse contribution

### Onset, not completion

The construction locates a left boundary: the event or state begins or becomes
active. It does not itself assert completion.

The resulting interval may continue, but continuation is not equivalent to the
separate continuative construction `V落去`.

### Manifestation reading

In examples such as:

```text
佢笑起上嚟好明顯。
佢野蠻起上嚟真係不可理喻㗎。
```

English translations naturally use “when X” rather than “X began.” The
construction introduces the interval in which a behavior or property is
manifest. This is related to onset but should not be reduced to a one-time
beginning event in every context.

### Triggered change

Many examples make the trigger explicit:

```text
聽到個消息之後
一句話引到
突然間
好快就
```

These help distinguish onset from a stable generic description. Triggered
contexts are evidence for the semantics but are not established as obligatory
syntax.

### Relation to lexical `開始`

Lexical `開始` also expresses beginning:

```text
佢開始打網球。
```

The current evidence does not show that `開始 + VP` and `V起上嚟` are freely
interchangeable. The directional-origin marker has different object placement,
discourse preferences, and manifestation uses. It therefore requires a separate
research boundary.

## Object-order boundary

### Source-supported object placement

The strongest supported object-bearing order is:

```text
V + 起 + OBJECT + (上)嚟
```

Examples:

```text
學起法文嚟
發起脾氣上嚟
打起網球上嚟
查起稅上嚟
```

### Unsupported or unresolved controls

The following were probed as boundary controls:

```text
佢打起上嚟網球。
佢打網球起上嚟。
佢發脾氣起上嚟。
```

They do not match the object-splitting pattern stated by the primary and
reference sources. This mismatch is not, by itself, a controlled native-speaker
judgment. They should therefore be treated as **source-incompatible or
unresolved boundary probes**, not published categorical ungrammaticalities.

### Discontinuity requirement

A future parser candidate must be capable of recognizing a discontinuous
construction whose host verb and object remain internally related.

A contiguous-token heuristic for `起上嚟` would miss the strongest transitive
profile. A broad search for any earlier `起` and later `上嚟` would overgenerate
literal caused-motion and unrelated material.

## Literal-directional boundary

Yiu gives literal motion examples:

```text
佢好快噉跳咗起嚟。
佢好快噉擰起本書嚟。
隻麻雀突然飛咗起嚟。
佢即刻就擰起本書嚟。
```

The semantic participants differ from inchoative aspect:

- a mover changes vertical position;
- an object is lifted;
- the path is upward;
- `嚟` contributes motion toward the speaker or deictic center.

Literal controls probed here include:

```text
佢企起上嚟。
佢拎起本書上嚟。
佢行上嚟。
佢將支竹戙起上嚟。
```

Surface similarity is insufficient. Future disambiguation must inspect:

- whether there is a physical mover or moved object;
- whether an upward path is plausible and asserted;
- whether the lexical verb already selects caused motion;
- whether the clause identifies an onset trigger or a spatial goal;
- whether the object is the theme of movement or the object of the lexical
  activity; and
- whether the expression participates in a higher temporal or evaluative frame.

## Evaluative and discourse boundaries

### `睇起嚟`

Example:

```text
呢件事睇起嚟好困難。
```

This has an appearance or preliminary-judgment reading, approximately “looks
or seems difficult.” It is not ordinary onset of seeing.

A future grammar may analyze it as lexicalized, constructional, or an
evaluative predicate. PRQ2-007 does not decide that question; it only excludes
it from the narrow onset core.

### `講起嚟`

Examples:

```text
件事講起嚟真係一匹布咁長。
講起嚟，我都識何先生。
```

These can mean “when one starts talking about it” or “speaking of that.” The
second is a discourse-topic trigger, not merely the beginning of speaking.

The current runtime already gives the second a broad clause-linking wrapper.
That wrapper does not resolve the internal `講起嚟` subtype.

### Generic behavior versus one-time onset

Example:

```text
佢笑起上嚟好明顯。
```

The interpretation may be generic “when he smiles” rather than a report of one
particular onset. The construction still introduces the active interval of
smiling, but a future parser must not force episodic past or present onset.

## Negation, modality, and aspect interaction

The checked sources establish the core marker and object placement more
strongly than they establish the full interaction inventory.

Unresolved questions include:

- whether external negation denies onset, denies the host event, or produces a
  prevention reading;
- which modals naturally scope over the onset construction;
- whether perfective `咗` can follow or scope over the aspectual profile and in
  which positions;
- whether `又` indicates repeated onset or simple recurrence;
- whether `一 … 就 …` is a separate correlative parent or part of the most
  characteristic constructional profile;
- whether `起嚟` and `起上嚟` differ in register or lexical compatibility;
- how aspectual onset interacts with potential `得／唔到`; and
- which final particles favor episodic versus generic readings.

Yiu's blocked potential example concerns a directional profile. It should not
be silently generalized into a complete prohibition for every aspectual use.

## Predicate compatibility

### Directly attested host types

The checked evidence includes:

- vocal or social activities: `喊`, `嘈`, `笑`;
- state or change predicates: `光`, `嬲`, `野蠻`, `轉`;
- learning or activity predicates with objects: `學法文`, `打網球`;
- verb-object compounds: `發脾氣`, `黐線`;
- institutional activity: `查稅`.

### Not yet established

The sources do not establish unrestricted compatibility with:

- punctual achievements that lack an extendable post-onset interval;
- inherently completed predicates;
- every adjective or property predicate;
- every verb-object compound;
- every motion verb;
- every perception verb;
- lexicalized `V起嚟` expressions; or
- arbitrary borrowed verbs and code-mixed objects.

A future productive analysis needs broader corpus diversity and blinded native
judgments across predicate classes.

## Orthography and form inventory

The central form is written:

```text
起上嚟
```

with Jyutping:

```text
hei2 soeng5 lai4
```

The related shorter form is:

```text
起嚟
hei2 lai4
```

Sources may use traditional `嚟`, standard-written `來`, or romanization-only
forms. A future lexical normalization layer may connect orthographic variants,
but source spelling should be preserved in evidence records.

`起身` is sometimes described pedagogically as an inchoative-related form, but
it also has strong lexical meanings such as getting up. It is outside the
supported core of this unit and requires independent boundary research before
being merged.

## Current runtime behavior at v0.5.213

### Repository overlap

The current repository has source-linked research notes for directional
families including:

```text
DirectionalMotionVP
CompoundDirectionalMotionVP
DirectedMannerMotionVP
NegatedDirectionalMotionVP
```

It does not have a dedicated inchoative `起上嚟` construction or research unit.

The lexicon and directional recognizers know `上` and `嚟` as motion material.
That creates a predictable collision: the tail `上嚟` or final `嚟` is wrapped
as direction while the host predicate and onset marker `起` remain unrelated.

### Probe method

One-off probes used the existing runtime functions:

```text
analyzeLine
 diagnosticFinalRows
 diagnosticSummary
 rootSpanCoverageForDiagnostic
```

against runtime `0.5.213`.

The probe set included:

- unsplit dynamic and stative targets;
- split object and verb-object targets;
- the shorter `起嚟` variant;
- source-incompatible object-order controls;
- literal upward-motion controls;
- evaluative and discourse uses; and
- a lexical `開始` control.

Runtime output has zero linguistic-evidence weight. It is used only to locate
collisions and missing structure.

### Systematic false directional tail

For most `起上嚟` targets, the only recognized construction is:

```text
DirectionalMotionVP: 上嚟
```

The runtime leaves `起` outside that construction and does not connect the
preceding predicate to an onset reading.

Examples include:

```text
佢好突然咁喊起上嚟。
我一嬲起上嚟就亂咁鬧人。
佢野蠻起上嚟真係不可理喻㗎。
佢笑起上嚟好明顯。
```

This is not merely missing a label. It assigns spatial-directional semantics to
material that the sources classify as aspectual or manifestation-related.

### Split-object failure

For:

```text
老闆發起脾氣上嚟好得人驚㗎。
佢打起網球上嚟成個張德培咁。
```

runtime recognizes only final `上嚟` as directional. It does not construct:

- the lexical host and object relation;
- the discontinuous `起 … 上嚟` marker; or
- the higher evaluation or subordinate relation.

For the source-incompatible control:

```text
佢發脾氣起上嚟。
```

runtime separately recognizes contiguous `發脾氣` as `ProductiveVO` and
`上嚟` as directional. It has no mechanism to compare this order with the
source-supported split profile.

### Generic wrapper masking

The shorter-form attestation:

```text
佢一句話引到成屋人都笑起嚟。
```

receives root `PASS` through `ReportedSpeech`, but its only internal marker
analysis is:

```text
DirectionalMotionVP: 嚟
```

Likewise:

```text
講起嚟，我都識何先生。
```

receives root `PASS` through `ClauseLinkingSequence`, while `嚟` remains a
false directional child.

Root coverage therefore cannot be used as evidence that onset or discourse
subtype has been represented.

### Literal control behavior

The runtime correctly gives:

```text
佢行上嚟。
```

full `SubjectPredicateClause` coverage with a `DirectedMannerMotionVP` child.
This is a genuine literal motion success that must be protected from any broad
future onset rule.

Other literal controls remain incomplete:

```text
佢企起上嚟。
佢拎起本書上嚟。
佢將支竹戙起上嚟。
```

They receive only a tail `DirectionalMotionVP: 上嚟` plus disconnected
material. Future work must therefore both add onset structure and improve or
preserve genuine motion structure; one cannot substitute for the other.

### Evaluative and discourse underanalysis

For:

```text
呢件事睇起嚟好困難。
```

runtime separately recognizes the topic NP, final `嚟` as directional, and
`好困難` as a degree-stative predicate. The evaluative dependency is absent.

For:

```text
件事講起嚟真係一匹布咁長。
```

runtime recognizes the NP and final `嚟`, but not the discourse/evaluation
relation.

These are not evidence for folding evaluative and discourse uses into the
inchoative core. They demonstrate neighboring unresolved constructions that a
future guard must avoid damaging.

## Collision map

### `DirectionalMotionVP`

Current behavior:

```text
上嚟
嚟
```

is wrapped as directional material even inside non-motion onset, appearance,
and discourse profiles.

Disposition:

- retain for genuine spatial/deictic motion;
- do not use it as a substitute for inchoative aspect;
- suppress or subordinate the false directional tail only inside independently
  licensed non-motion constructions; and
- preserve diagnostic visibility when disambiguation is unresolved.

### `DirectedMannerMotionVP`

Current behavior correctly covers `行上嚟`.

Disposition:

- protect genuine motion verbs and upward/deictic paths;
- require stronger evidence before assigning onset to motion-compatible hosts;
- allow a higher ambiguous or dual analysis only where context genuinely
  supports both readings.

### `ProductiveVO`

Current behavior recognizes contiguous `發脾氣` but not the split
`發起脾氣上嚟` profile.

Disposition:

- preserve the lexical verb-object dependency across discontinuity;
- do not require a contiguous host span;
- do not accept arbitrary `V起N上嚟` without lexical or valency support.

### `ClauseLinkingSequence`, `ReportedSpeech`, and other wrappers

Current higher wrappers can produce root `PASS` while the internal construction
is missing or wrong.

Disposition:

- higher discourse and clause relations may remain parents;
- they must not erase the inchoative, manifestation, evaluative, or discourse
  subtype of the marked member.

### Change-state and result nodes

The onset meaning is related to change of state but not identical to a lexical
result complement or `變成`-type change.

Disposition:

- do not merge solely because both involve change;
- distinguish the transition into an event/state from a result state selected
  by the lexical verb;
- test whether a future inchoative node should be aspectual, relational, or a
  subtype family.

## Required guardrails for any future implementation

### Positive gates

Require evidence for:

- a host predicate compatible with onset or manifestation;
- contiguous `V/A + 起上嚟` or `V/A + 起嚟`; or
- a source-supported split `V + 起 + OBJECT + (上)嚟` profile;
- preservation of verb-object or valency relations;
- the distinction between internal onset and higher clause relation; and
- exact Jyutping and orthographic mapping.

### Negative guards

Protect or separately classify:

```text
行上嚟
跳咗起嚟
擰起本書嚟
戙起上嚟
睇起嚟
講起嚟
開始 + VP
起身
```

Do not infer aspect merely from:

- final `上嚟`;
- an earlier token `起` somewhere in the clause;
- a following adjective;
- root `PASS` from a generic wrapper; or
- a generated example that matches a surface template.

### Object guards

For split objects:

- verify that the object belongs to the lexical host;
- reject accidental capture across clause boundaries;
- avoid treating locative goals as host objects;
- preserve classifier and NP structure;
- distinguish moved themes from ordinary activity objects; and
- do not infer acceptability of unsupported word orders from runtime coverage.

## Further evidence needed

### Adult corpus expansion

Search independently for:

- unsplit dynamic onset with multiple verbs;
- state and adjective onset;
- split transitive objects;
- split verb-object compounds;
- `起嚟` versus `起上嚟` distribution;
- episodic versus generic manifestation readings;
- negation and modal scope;
- co-occurrence with `咗`, `又`, and final particles;
- regional Guangzhou, Hong Kong, Macau, and overseas usage; and
- literal motion minimal contrasts using the same lexical verbs.

Corpus hits must be reviewed in context. Surface counts of `起上嚟` are not
sufficient because motion, evaluative, and discourse uses overlap.

### Blinded native-speaker panel

Before drafting an instrument, the user must provide the survey-generation
prompt required by project policy.

A future blinded survey should test matched contrasts without revealing a
preferred answer. It should separate:

- naturalness;
- onset versus generic manifestation interpretation;
- literal upward motion;
- object position;
- `起嚟` versus `起上嚟`;
- discourse `講起嚟`;
- evaluative `睇起嚟`; and
- context dependence.

## Research conclusion

Cantonese `起上嚟` and the related shorter `起嚟` have a directly documented
inchoative use marking the beginning or manifestation of an event or state.
The strongest transitive evidence is discontinuous: `V起O上嚟` or `V起O嚟`.
The construction is especially prominent in subordinate, evaluative, and
“once/when X manifests” frames, but those higher relations should remain
separate from the internal onset dependency.

The same surface material remains active in literal upward/deictic motion, and
short `起嚟` also occurs in appearance and discourse-linking expressions. A
single unrestricted directional or aspect rule is therefore unsupported.

Runtime v0.5.213 has no dedicated inchoative representation. It systematically
wraps only `上嚟` or `嚟` as directional, misses split objects, and can hide the
defect under broad root-level wrappers. This establishes a concrete research
and collision gap, not authorization to change runtime behavior.
