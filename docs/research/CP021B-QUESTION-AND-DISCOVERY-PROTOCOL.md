# CP021B Question and Discovery Protocol

> Lifecycle event: **QUESTION_FREEZE**  
> Status: **IN_PROGRESS / NO LANGUAGE CLAIM / NO RUNTIME AUTHORIZATION**  
> Frozen: 2026-07-17

## Neutral observable question

In independently documented or naturally attested modern Hong Kong Cantonese clauses containing `畀` or `俾`, what form–meaning patterns occur when two or more overt participants are present, and what evidence bears on their linear order, predicate dependence, participant interpretation, optionality, ambiguity, and discourse completeness?

This wording does not assume that the old Canto Span `RecipientFrame` is valid, that transfer and beneficiary patterns must be separate or unified, or that any source's syntactic theory must become the parser representation.

## Why the question was opened

The active queue contains a high-impact legacy parser wrapper around some `畀/俾` spans. That internal fact selects an area needing research; it supplies no Cantonese hypothesis and no preferred replacement inventory.

## Observable scope

Include clauses or context-bearing fragments in which:

- `畀` or `俾` is overt;
- at least two participant expressions are overt in the clause or recoverable local context;
- the marker may be the main predicate, occur after another predicate, or precede another predicate;
- participant order, predicate class, aspect, negation, following event material, possession, or discourse context may affect interpretation.

Primary variety is contemporary Hong Kong Cantonese. Historical, diaspora, Taishanese, Mandarin, and other Yue evidence may be retained only as explicitly bounded comparison, never silently pooled.

## Pre-registered research dimensions

1. surface order of predicate, theme-like material, marker, and participant;
2. whether `畀/俾` is described as a lexical predicate, grammatical marker, serial element, coverb, or another category;
3. lexical or semantic restrictions on the preceding predicate;
4. interpretation of the post-marker participant;
5. alternations or optionality reported for the same event type;
6. attachment and scope of aspect and negation;
7. possessed-NP and retained-object ambiguities;
8. passive, permissive, instrument, quotation, and fragment/non-target boundaries;
9. register, period, speaker, and orthographic variation;
10. competing analyses and direct counterexamples.

These dimensions are questions, not predicted answers.

## Source classes to search

Priority order is evidential, not agreement-based:

1. primary descriptive or theoretical research with explicit Cantonese examples and exact locators;
2. peer-reviewed corpus or experimental studies with recoverable methods;
3. established reference grammars with identifiable authors and editions;
4. public Cantonese corpora with recoverable context and provenance;
5. theses or conference papers when the primary evidence is inspectable;
6. dictionaries only for lexical corroboration, never construction productivity;
7. secondary summaries only as discovery leads.

## Inclusion criteria

A source candidate is included for proposition extraction only if it provides at least one of:

- an explicit modern Cantonese example relevant to the observable scope;
- an explicit distributional, semantic, or syntactic statement with a locator;
- recoverable natural records with relevant context and provenance;
- a directly relevant competing analysis or restriction;
- a bounded historical/variety contrast that prevents overgeneralization.

Agreement with existing project behavior is not an inclusion criterion.

## Exclusion criteria

- wrong language or variety without an explicit Cantonese comparison;
- search snippets without recoverable source text;
- unsourced teaching pages, generated summaries, or forum assertions;
- duplicate copies that add no independent evidence;
- examples lacking enough context to identify the relevant surface pattern;
- sources cited only for the parser's label or ontology;
- inaccessible sources whose relevant proposition cannot be verified.

Every serious candidate is logged even when excluded or inaccessible.

## Discovery stop conditions

Source screening may freeze only after:

- the registered English and Chinese concept families have been searched;
- reference lists and terminology from included primary sources have been followed for serious candidates;
- at least descriptive/theoretical, reference-grammar, and natural-corpus source classes have been attempted;
- explicit searches for counterexamples, alternations, ambiguity, and non-target boundaries have been run;
- additional queries cease adding materially different primary propositions or analyses;
- every serious candidate has an inclusion/access decision.

These are coverage conditions, not a quota guaranteeing truth.
