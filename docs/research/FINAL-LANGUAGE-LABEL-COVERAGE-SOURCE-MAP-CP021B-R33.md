# Final unmapped language-label source map (CP021B-R33)

Date: 2026-07-19  
Scope: claim-level research mapping only. **R33 changes no parser grammar, lexicon, Jyutping, fixture, manifest data, accepted behavior, or held-out file.** The LX1 lexical candidate remains inherited, render-pending, and unaccepted.

## Why these five labels are mapped together

R32 left exactly five unmapped language-layer labels. Two are bounded relation/motion claims (`IntendedFunctionRelation`, `MotionDelimitedVP`), one is a dormant caused-motion label (`DirectionalCausedMotionVP`), and two are broad implementation umbrellas (`ProductiveVO`, `TransitiveVP`). Mapping them together closes the language-claim provenance queue without pretending that the umbrella labels are single Cantonese constructions.

## Runtime screen

- `DirectionalCausedMotionVP` is dormant: registry metadata exists, but no active constructor or fallback and no inspected accepted-tree occurrence were found.
- `IntendedFunctionRelation` actively wraps a broad resource/topic + optional clause material + `用嚟` + predicate sequence and already records actual-use/affordance ambiguity.
- `MotionDelimitedVP` cross-products movement verb + action verb + `吓`; it has no inspected accepted-tree occurrence.
- `ProductiveVO` and `TransitiveVP` recur broadly as transparent wrappers across many accepted trees and runtime branches. Their reach is migration-impact metadata, not linguistic evidence.

## Source-derived conclusions

### Directional caused motion

Yiu studies a bounded Cantonese directional-verb family and explicitly states that the directional element compounds with the preceding verb in causative use but not in ordinary directional use. This supports a real causative/directional boundary. It does not support the dormant Canto Span node, exact slots, or a generic caused-motion cross-product. `DirectionalCausedMotionVP` is therefore a merge-or-retire label whose underlying relations must remain independently typed.

### `用嚟` and intended function

Wong gives the exact example `呢個用嚟切嘢`, but analyzes it under instrumental serial-verb constructions together with `攞`, `用`, `搵`, and the `愛嚟/攞嚟/用嚟` profiles. The instrument may be omitted or moved to subject position. The runtime must therefore preserve overt resource structure and distinguish intended function from actual use event, affordance, ordinary lexical `用`, and other `嚟` linkers.

### Motion, purpose, and delimitative `吓`

Wong defines purpose SVCs and gives `我去(書店)買書` plus `買本書(嚟)睇吓`. The latter shows optional purpose linker `嚟` and delimitative `吓` attached to the second action. It does not attest the runtime's `movement_verb + action_verb + 吓` cross-product. Future remediation should compose a purpose relation with an independently licensed delimited VP.

### VO and transitivity umbrellas

Wong defines a descriptive VO profile as verb followed by object, but the same coding bucket includes copular, existential, and ditransitive forms with recoverable omissions. Its SV profile includes both intransitive verbs and transitive verbs with dropped objects. Alderete et al. separately distinguish intransitive, transitive, and ditransitive VP schemata.

Consequently:

- `ProductiveVO` is not an independently justified productive Cantonese construction. The word *productive* in GACS is a developmental measurement concept, not a parser-generation license.
- `TransitiveVP` may remain a transparent internal grouping, but only after lexeme/use valency, complement type, null-argument licensing, relative-gap provenance, and ditransitive structure are established.

## Forbidden inferences

- Code references and accepted-tree occurrences are not external evidence.
