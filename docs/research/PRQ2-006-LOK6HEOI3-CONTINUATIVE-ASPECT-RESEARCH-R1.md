# PRQ2-006 — directional-aspectual `V落去` continuation

## Research decision

Create one dedicated research unit for Cantonese postpredicative `落去 lok6
heoi3` when it presents an activity, process, or state as continuing beyond a
contextually relevant reference point.

The strongest supported core is:

```text
SUBJECT / TOPIC + (MODAL / ADVERBIAL) + PREDICATE + 落去
```

with an optional following clause that states a projected consequence,
evaluation, or unresolved future development.

Directly checked examples include:

```text
你講落去咯。
你要再堅持落去呀。
公司咁樣蝕落去唔得嘅。
就照你咁樣做落去。
你一直講落去喇。
再凍落去，啲花就會死。
件事發展落去，唔知道會點呢？
你做住落去先啦。
你可以捱得落去嗎？
```

The supported contribution is not simply “ongoing now.” It directs attention
to the event or state **continuing from a reference point into a later interval**.
This distinguishes it from:

- progressive `緊`, which locates an event in progress;
- continuous or durative `住`, which presents an activity or state as being
  maintained without change;
- literal compound directional `落去`, which encodes downward motion and
  movement away from the speaker;
- lexical predicate `落去`, “go down”; and
- result or state uses historically related to the directional source.

The evidence also establishes that continuative `落去` retains several
properties unlike a fully generalized monosyllabic aspect suffix. In the core
profile it remains adjacent to the predicate, normally does not license an
overt object after it, can participate in potential expressions, and may
co-occur with another aspect marker such as `住`.

This note authorizes no parser change, registry change, construction-status
change, lexicon installation, acceptance fixture, survey instrument, or master
backlog edit.

## Independent evidence base

### A. Dedicated grammaticalization study

Yiu (2016) is the central source. The chapter explicitly identifies modern
Cantonese `落去` after a predicate as a continuative marker expressing either:

- continuation of an action; or
- maintenance of a state.

It analyzes a pathway from concrete direction through result/state to aspect,
while emphasizing that directional and aspectual uses remain synchronically
available.

The chapter's direct modern examples include:

```text
你講落去咯！
你要再堅持落去呀！
```

Its checked historical examples include:

```text
就照你咁樣做落去。
你一直講落去喇。
就自己辦落去。
```

The historical examples establish attestation and a documented development
path. They do not by themselves establish unrestricted productivity in every
modern variety.

### B. University-hosted reference grammar

The multimedia supplement to Matthews and Yip's comprehensive grammar lists
`落去` under “inchoative and continuative” and supplies:

```text
公司咁樣蝕落去唔得嘅。
```

The gloss treats `蝕落去` as “go on losing.” This independently confirms a
non-motion predicate with a continuative interpretation.

### C. Cantonese lexical resource

Words.hk records two distinct entries for the same written form:

1. a suffix placed after a verb to indicate continuation into the future;
2. a lexical verb meaning movement from a higher to a lower place.

Its continuative examples include:

```text
你再咁樣打落去會死㗎！
再咁落去，一定冇機會升返上D1。
```

Its directional control is:

```text
落去樓下買報紙。
```

This resource is valuable for modern usage and lexical contrast. It is not a
controlled acceptability study and does not independently prove the limits of
every syntactic profile.

## Narrow supported profiles

### 1. Dynamic activity continuation

```text
V-ACTIVITY + 落去
```

Examples:

```text
你講落去咯。
你要再堅持落去呀。
就照你咁樣做落去。
你一直講落去喇。
你再咁樣打落去會死㗎。
```

The predicate denotes an activity or process that can extend through time.
`落去` instructs the addressee or presents the situation as continuing beyond
an already relevant stage.

The construction does not assert that the event began at speech time. The
starting point may be:

- the speech time;
- an earlier discourse-established interval;
- a hypothetical future point; or
- a reference point introduced by a conditional or modal frame.

### 2. Process or state continuation

Yiu explicitly includes states remaining in force. The reference grammar gives
a process-state predicate:

```text
公司咁樣蝕落去唔得嘅。
```

Words.hk gives an ellipsis-like state profile:

```text
再咁落去，一定冇機會升返上D1。
```

The second example lacks an overt lexical verb immediately before `落去`.
`咁` recovers the manner or state from prior discourse. This is evidence for a
licensed discourse-dependent profile, not evidence for a zero-restriction
suffix attachable to any preceding material.

### 3. Conditional or projected-consequence frame

Modern examples include:

```text
再凍落去，啲花就會死。
件事發展落去，唔知道會點呢？
你再咁樣打落去會死㗎。
```

The first member supplies a continued situation; the following member evaluates
or predicts its consequences. Yiu describes modern `落去` in examples such as
`再凍落去` and `件事發展落去` as introducing subsequent new information.

A future analysis therefore needs to distinguish at least:

- the internal continuative predicate;
- the higher clause relation; and
- whether the second clause is conditional consequence, evaluation, or open
  projection.

A generic clause wrapper must not erase the continuative child.

### 4. Co-occurrence with continuous `住`

Yiu cites:

```text
你做住落去先啦！
```

This is important negative evidence against treating `落去` as interchangeable
with `住`.

The two markers contribute different temporal perspectives:

- `住` maintains an activity or state;
- `落去` projects its continuation beyond the reference point.

The exact ordering is source-supported in this example. The evidence does not
authorize every aspect-marker combination or every ordering.

### 5. Potential expression

Yiu reports:

```text
你可以捱得落去嗎？
```

and notes that `落去` can occur in a potential pattern. This retained
complement-like behavior is one reason some analyses resist classifying it as a
fully ordinary aspect suffix.

The surface string may be ambiguous between literal directional ability and
continuative endurance for motion-compatible verbs. Predicate semantics and
context must therefore be part of the analysis.

## Object and adjacency restriction

The strongest checked description says that continuative `落去` stays adjacent
to the predicate and cannot be split by an object.

Yiu marks the following as unacceptable for the intended continuative reading:

```text
*佢會一直講落去故事。
*佢會一直講落故事去。
```

while accepting:

```text
佢會一直講落去。
```

This does not mean that a transitive lexical predicate can never participate in
a continuative proposition. It means that the checked core does not license an
overt post-`落去` object or an object splitting the two syllables in these
patterns.

Possible strategies requiring separate research include:

- object omission when recoverable;
- topic-fronting;
- a different verb-object construction;
- nominal incorporation or lexicalized compounds; and
- dialectal or discourse-conditioned variation.

No such strategy is generalized here.

## Literal-directional boundary

Literal compound directional `落去` combines downward path with deictic movement
away from the speaker. Checked examples include:

```text
佢行咗落去樓下。
佢擰咗本書落去樓下。
隻麻雀突然飛咗落去。
我行落去。
佢落咗去。
```

These profiles differ from continuative aspect because a physical figure moves
along a spatial path. The moving figure may be:

- the subject in self-motion;
- an object in caused motion; or
- the understood argument of lexical `落去`.

The surface sequence `V落去` is therefore insufficient for classification.
At minimum, analysis must inspect:

1. whether the predicate denotes motion or caused motion;
2. whether a physical mover is available;
3. whether a location, path, or deictic endpoint is expressed or recoverable;
4. whether temporal continuation is independently supported by discourse;
5. whether an aspect marker such as `咗` separates the manner verb from the
   directional complement; and
6. whether the string has a plausible non-spatial continuation reading.

### Ambiguous motion-compatible verbs

```text
我行落去。
```

naturally supports a literal “walk down/away” reading in the existing project.
In a suitable discourse, an English translation like “keep walking” may also be
conceivable, but the checked sources do not establish that exact isolated
surface as a productive continuative example.

Do not override a well-supported directional analysis merely because `落去`
has an aspectual use elsewhere.

## Result/state and discourse-projection boundaries

Yiu documents a historical semantic path:

```text
direction > result/state > aspect > introduction of following information
```

This is a grammaticalization relationship, not proof that all synchronic uses
belong to one undifferentiated node.

Examples such as the following require careful distinction:

```text
再凍落去，啲花就會死。
件事發展落去，唔知道會點呢？
```

They contain continuation but also organize the following proposition. A future
ontology may require:

- a continuative-aspect child;
- a discourse-projection relation; or
- a typed clause-linking construction preserving both.

The present research does not choose among those implementation designs.

## Predicate compatibility

The evidence supports at least:

- speech and communication: `講`;
- persistence/endurance: `堅持`, `捱`;
- general activity: `做`, `辦`, `打`;
- financial/process change: `蝕`, `發展`;
- scalar state change: `凍`;
- discourse-recovered manner/state: `咁`.

The sources do not provide a complete lexical class. Tentative semantic
requirements are:

- the situation must be temporally extendable;
- a later interval must be conceptually available;
- continuation must be pragmatically coherent; and
- telic predicates may require coercion, iteration, or focus on an internal
  process rather than a completed endpoint.

Atomic achievements, instantaneous changes, and predicates whose lexical
meaning already fixes a terminal endpoint require controlled native-speaker and
corpus testing before inclusion.

## Comparison with neighboring aspect constructions

### `緊` progressive

```text
V緊
```

locates an event as ongoing at a reference time. It does not by itself instruct
that the event continue into a later interval.

### `住` continuous/durative

```text
V住
```

presents an activity or state as maintained. `V住落去` shows that maintained
state and forward continuation can co-occur.

### `開` habitual/established activity

```text
V開
```

presents a recurrent or established pattern. `落去` instead concerns the
forward continuation of the current or contextually selected situation.

### lexical `繼續`

The lexical verb/adverb `繼續` explicitly means “continue.” It may paraphrase
some `V落去` clauses, but lexical paraphrase does not make it the same
construction. Their argument structure, word order, aspect combinations, and
discourse behavior require separate analysis.

## Runtime collision audit: v0.5.213

Direct observation used `analyzeLine`, `diagnosticFinalRows`, and root-span
coverage against runtime 0.5.213. Runtime output has zero linguistic-evidence
weight.

### Systematic false directional decomposition

Most continuative targets are analyzed through combinations such as:

```text
VerbComplementVP: 講落去
VerbComplementVP: 落去
DirectionalMotionVP: 去
```

The runtime therefore recognizes local verb-complement material but assigns the
final `去` a directional-motion role. It has no dedicated continuative analysis
for the full `V落去` unit.

Affected sourced examples include:

```text
你講落去咯。
你要再堅持落去呀。
就照你咁樣做落去。
你一直講落去喇。
再凍落去，啲花就會死。
件事發展落去，唔知道會點呢？
```

### Generic wrapper masking

Several examples receive full root coverage through higher constructions:

- `SubjectModalPredicateClause`;
- `ModalVP`;
- `ClauseLinkingSequence`;
- `ClauseRelation`; or
- `NeedsContext`.

The full-span result is not a correct continuative analysis. The relevant child
remains absent or directional.

### False acceptance of source-marked object patterns

Both source-marked negative boundaries receive full runtime coverage:

```text
佢會一直講落去故事。
佢會一直講落故事去。
```

The runtime's broad modal and verb-complement wrappers therefore fail to enforce
the documented adjacency/object boundary.

### Failure on `住落去`

```text
你做住落去先啦。
```

receives only a partial `PriorityMarkerClause` over `去先啦`; `做住落去` is not
represented as a composition of continuous and continuative aspect.

### Literal controls remain inconsistent

```text
我行落去。
```

reaches `DirectedMannerMotionVP`, preserving a literal directional profile.
Other sourced literal examples such as `佢行咗落去樓下` and `佢擰咗本書落去樓下`
are only partially represented and often reduce `落去` to generic
`VerbComplementVP` plus `DirectionalMotionVP: 去`.

The future task is therefore not merely to add an aspect label. It must also
preserve and repair the genuine directional family without allowing aspectual
`落去` to overwrite it.

## Research-only structural requirements

Any future design should satisfy all of the following before implementation:

1. Recognize a predicate-hosted continuative dependency over the whole `V落去`
   sequence.
2. Preserve the host predicate as the event or state being continued.
3. Distinguish literal downward/deictic motion using semantic and syntactic
   evidence rather than character identity.
4. Preserve higher modal, conditional, and clause-relation wrappers as parents,
   not substitutes.
5. Reject or quarantine source-marked object placement after or inside
   continuative `落去`.
6. Represent `V住落去` composition without collapsing `住` and `落去`.
7. Preserve potential forms without treating every `V得落去` as aspectual.
8. Keep lexical `落去` and compound-directional `V落去` available.
9. Avoid promoting a broad productive rule from dictionary examples alone.
10. Record regional, lexical, and discourse variation in held-out evidence.

## Controlled contrasts required before implementation

A future evidence packet should include matched lexical frames for:

### Aspect versus literal direction

```text
你一直講落去。
佢一直行落去樓下。
```

The exact lexical and contextual matching must be improved before survey use.

### Continuative versus progressive

```text
佢講緊。
佢講落去。
```

Context must make both intended readings pragmatically available.

### Continuative versus continuous

```text
你做住先。
你做落去先。
你做住落去先。
```

### Object placement

```text
佢會一直講落去。
*佢會一直講落去故事。
*佢會一直講落故事去。
```

The last two are source-marked for the intended reading. Native-speaker evidence
should confirm current regional judgments rather than merely repeat the
publication.

### Consequence relation

```text
再凍落去，啲花就會死。
件事發展落去，唔知道會點呢？
```

These test different following-clause relations while retaining continuation.

## Unresolved questions

1. Which modern predicates freely license continuative `落去`?
2. How strongly must the event already be underway?
3. Can stative predicates license the form without scalar change?
4. Which telic predicates permit process coercion or iteration?
5. Are postverbal objects categorically blocked in all modern varieties, or can
   topic structure or prosody license apparent counterexamples?
6. What is the exact analysis of `咁落去` without an overt lexical verb?
7. Should consequence-projecting examples receive a dedicated discourse
   relation above continuative aspect?
8. How do `唔`, `未`, modal verbs, and A-not-A questions scope over the form?
9. Which aspect markers can precede `落去`, and in what order?
10. When does `V得／唔落去` express potential continuation versus physical
    directional possibility?
11. Can speech context disambiguate motion-compatible hosts such as `行`?
12. How much regional variation exists between Hong Kong, Guangzhou, Macau,
    Pearl River Delta, and heritage varieties?
