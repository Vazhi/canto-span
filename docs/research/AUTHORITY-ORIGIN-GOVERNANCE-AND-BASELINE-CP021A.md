## Directive corrections

The following permanent rules were detrimental and are superseded:

- indefinite freeze on all new grammar;
- one-for-one replacement as the only path to new coverage;
- preservation of accepted fixtures as a linguistic presumption;
- treating an unchecked native-review box as affirmative naturalness evidence;
- prohibiting all standalone learner-semantics corrections;
- allowing a large research batch to remain unexported until its final disposition.

The narrower replacements preserve stability without blocking genuine Cantonese coverage: legacy quarantine, authority-origin future research, explicit native-review states, regression as a change detector, learner-harm exceptions, and event-based recovery exports.

## Anti-bias safeguards

1. Freeze an observable, neutral question before searching.
2. Record discovery terms, source classes, inclusion criteria, and all serious candidate-source decisions.
3. Extract propositions and counterevidence before writing a project claim.
4. Keep exact locators, limitations, register, variety, and source independence on every edge.
5. Label inference as inference; do not turn source terminology into a required parser ontology.
6. Treat non-recovery as query-limited unless the search design justifies more.
7. Freeze evaluation only after the source-derived claim exists; keep implementation separate.
8. Export recovery artifacts at question, screen, extraction, synthesis, and pre-implementation milestones.

## Runtime disposition
