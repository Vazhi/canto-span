## Why this is a dependency program

Broad valency reaches into aspect, completion, result, motion-purpose, serial action, cognition, speech, and fragment parsing. Replacing the umbrella in one place without mapping its consumers would either break accepted behavior or silently transfer the same unsupported claim to a new label.

## Frozen migration classes

1. **Direct umbrella templates** — `ProductiveVO`, `TransitiveVP`, `IntransitiveVP`: retire as public language claims after dependents migrate.
2. **Slot consumers** — templates requiring `action_verb`, `object`, `productive_vo`, `transitive_vp`, or `intransitive_vp`: each needs an exact construction-specific selection packet.
3. **Lexicon tags** — `transitive_affordance`, `transitive_verb`, `intransitive_verb`, `ditransitive_verb`, `action_verb`: retain as heuristics only until replaced by sourced predicate-use records.
4. **Surface seed table** — the 27 `PRODUCTIVE_VO` entries are useful regression and research seeds, but a curated list does not prove a general productive syntax.
5. **Direct constructors and fallback paths** — any manual construction call must be migrated or guarded so it cannot bypass the exact-family evidence layer.

## Promotion consequence

Families dependent on this map may still promote through a narrow subtype when their own packet explicitly supplies predicate sense, argument relation, object/complement category, omission policy, and negative boundaries. They must not inherit productivity from the generic valency tags.

## Next implementation-safe step

Rank the mapped consumers by accepted-fixture impact and family dependencies. Migrate only one exact relation at a time after visible packet lock and external prospective custody. Retire the broad labels only when no accepted dependent relies on their independent licensing.
