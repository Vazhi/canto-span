## Result

Several earlier instructions were protective at the time but are detrimental if kept as permanent rules. The table below supersedes them only to the stated extent.

| Prior directive | Risk to real Cantonese parsing | CP021A disposition |
|---|---|---|
| Freeze all new grammar indefinitely | Eventually blocks genuine constructions that emerge from independent evidence and preserves known gaps. | Replace with two tracks: legacy quarantine/audit plus authority-origin future grammar under strict gates. No grammar change is made in CP021A. |
| Permit additions only as one-for-one replacements for rejected labels | Encourages artificial mapping between unrelated analyses and can block valid new coverage. | Retire. Replacement remains preferred when correcting an invalid claim, but is not a precondition for independently originated grammar. |
| Preserve accepted behavior unless a concrete contradiction exists | Useful as a stability default, but fixtures can preserve invented grammar and internal expectations are not linguistic evidence. | Narrow. Regression detects change; external evidence can justify deliberate fixture retirement or rebaselining. |
| Do not reopen accepted A1 on audit warnings alone | Prevents churn from weak warnings, but would suppress source-backed contradictions. | Narrow. Do not reopen on internal warning alone; do reopen when pattern-specific external evidence materially contradicts the claim. |
| Assume unchecked native-screening items are natural | Conflates absence of a mark with an affirmative judgment and creates acquiescence/completion bias. | Retire for evidence. Require explicit natural / unnatural / unsure-or-context / not-seen status. |
| One checkbox per native item | Low friction, but cannot distinguish natural from skipped or uncertain. | Retire as an evidential design. Optional correction remains optional. |
| One coherent substantive batch may remain unexported | A large coherent audit can still hit conversation limits before downloads. | Replace with event-based interim exports at question freeze, source-screen freeze, extraction completion, synthesis, and pre-implementation. |
| Learner-role changes must never drive a standalone batch | Usually avoids low-value polish, but can leave a materially false learner explanation active. | Narrow. Cosmetic role work remains bundled; materially misleading or false learner semantics may justify an independent correction. |
| All claims must have an authoritative external source, read literally | External citations cannot source internal code facts or dictate parser ontology; underdescribed spoken behavior could be lost. | Clarify. Every statement needs provenance; Cantonese language claims need external evidence, internal engineering claims need exact internal provenance, and independent natural data may originate a claim. |
| Source authority decides the parser analysis | Would replace parser invention with authority selection and conceal scholarly disagreement. | Reject. Sources originate the behavior; Canto Span may use a transparent theory-neutral representation while preserving alternatives. |
| Autonomous work should continue until only an unavailable dependency remains | Can amplify a mistaken research direction or accumulate unexported work. | Retain only behind question registration, evidence gates, event-based recovery exports, and bounded stop conditions. |
| Summary-first, then inspect required/suspicious full diagnostics | Reduces review cost without treating summaries as semantic truth. | Retain. High-risk candidate-specific contracts may still require every full diagnostic. |
| Slim validation surface; absorb historical focused runners | Reduces maintenance noise while preserving aggregate behavior. | Retain. Add one durable provenance audit, not one runner per claim. |
| No rigid small-batch caps | Avoids fragmented research and arbitrary source limits. | Retain, but event-based recovery exports are mandatory within long coherent audits. |
| Rendered Obsidian review for learner-facing changes | Headless output cannot establish final UI correctness. | Retain. It validates rendering only, not grammar legitimacy. |

## Highest-risk conflict

The combination of post-hoc source search, indefinite freeze, regression preservation, and replacement-only implementation could produce a parser that is stable but conservative in the wrong ontology: invented legacy labels remain while new evidence is used mainly to rationalize or minimally reshape them. The authority-origin lifecycle replaces that direction.

## Instructions not treated as detrimental

- separate language, attestation, representation, heuristic, and lexical claims;
- parser success and historical acceptance are not grammatical truth;
- source-specific scope and competing analyses must remain visible;
- corpus hits require context and provenance;
- native naturalness is not structural analysis;
- downloads, ledgers, checksums, and resumable handoff are mandatory;
- learner-facing display and internal diagnostics may differ transparently;
- no parser behavior changes are authorized by a research or governance checkpoint alone.
